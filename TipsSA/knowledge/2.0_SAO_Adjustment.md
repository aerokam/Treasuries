# 2.0 SA with Outlier Adjustment (SAO)

## Overview
While the standard SA transform (Ask -> SA) removes predictable monthly seasonality, the resulting "SA Yield" curve can still exhibit significant "wiggles" or outliers, particularly at the front end (short-dated maturities). These are often caused by short-term market idiosyncraticies or specific seasonal anomalies that the standard factors don't fully capture.

The **SAO Adjustment** applies an empirical smoothing algorithm to the SA curve to identify the underlying real-yield trend.

## Backwards-Anchored Trend Fitting
The algorithm processes the yield curve from **longest maturity to shortest** (Right-to-Left).

### 1. The Anchor (Long End)
For bonds with maturities > 7 years, the market is typically efficient enough that SA Yields are stable. In this region:
\[ SAO = SA \]

### 2. The Sliding Window (Medium to Short End)
As the algorithm moves toward shorter maturities, it uses a sliding window of the next 4 longer bonds to establish a projected trend line (Linear Regression).

- **Projected Yield**: The intercept of the linear regression established by the longer bonds at the current bond's maturity date.
- **Blending**: The final SAO yield is a weighted blend of this Projected trend and the bond's actual SA Yield.

### 3. Blend Weights
The `trendWeight` (how much to trust the longer-term trend vs. the current bond's SA yield) varies by maturity:
- **Very Short (< 0.5 years)**: 90% Trend (Highly smoothed).
- **Short (0.5 to 2 years)**: 50% Trend.
- **Medium (2 to 5 years)**: 40% Trend.
- **Long (> 5 years)**: follows SA directly.

### 4. Monotonicity Constraint
To ensure a realistic yield curve, a strict monotonicity constraint is applied:
\[ SAO_i = \min(SAO_i, SAO_{i+1}) \]
(Assuming a positive yield curve slope).

### 5. Front-End Specific Fix
Specifically for the Apr 2026 vs Jul 2026 maturities, the algorithm forces a minimum downward slope of **0.5 bps** if the yields are too close, ensuring the shortest end of the curve remains logically anchored.
