# 1.0 Seasonal Adjustments (SA)

## Overview
Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities (TIPS) yields are calculated based on the "Clean Price," which does not include the inflation adjustment component. However, since TIPS are indexed to the Non-Seasonally Adjusted CPI (CPI-NSA), their monthly performance is subject to predictable seasonal patterns (e.g., higher inflation in spring, lower in autumn).

The **Seasonal Adjustment (SA)** transform aims to normalize these yields by applying monthly SA factors, allowing for a clearer comparison of real yields across different months of the year.

## The Transformation
The transformation converts a market **Ask Price** to a **Seasonally Adjusted Price**, which is then used to derive the **SA Yield**.

### 1. Price Adjustment Formula
\[ P_{SA} = P_{Ask} \times \frac{S_{settle}}{S_{maturity}} \]

Where:
- \( P_{SA} \): Seasonally Adjusted Price.
- \( P_{Ask} \): Raw Market Ask Price.
- \( S_{settle} \): SA Factor for the settlement date.
- \( S_{maturity} \): SA Factor for the maturity date.

### 2. Yield Derivation
The **SA Yield** is derived from \( P_{SA} \) using the standard bond yield-to-maturity (YTM) formula (Actual/Actual day count convention, semi-annual compounding).

## Data Sources
- **Ask Prices**: Sourced from FedInvest (daily) or uploaded via Broker CSV (Schwab/Fidelity).
- **SA Factors**: Calculated monthly from BLS CPI-U data (NSA vs SA components) and hosted in the TIPS Portal R2 bucket.
