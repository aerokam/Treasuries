## Purpose

Rebalance an existing TIPS ladder to align with a desired amount target (DARA). Two modes:
- **Gap**: Minimal trades; sells excess bracket TIPS to fund newly available maturities (formerly gaps).
- **Full**: Rebuilds the entire ladder from the current portfolio's total cash value, resetting to a clean DARA target.

## Context

TIPS ladders require periodic rebalancing as new TIPS are issued and gaps are filled. Originally, gaps (years with no TIPS) were covered by additional TIPS in bracket years. As new TIPS are issued, rebalancing sells this additional amount to buy the new maturities. Additionally, market price changes may drift the ladder's income profile; a full rebalance resets the entire ladder to a clean DARA target using the current portfolio value.

## Rebalancing-Specific Concepts

### Official Ladder Range

- **firstYear**: Earliest year with holdings
- **lastYear**: Latest contiguous year with holdings before any missing rung above 2040.
- Missing rungs above 2040 truncate the official range (e.g., holdings in 2047 and 2049 but not 2048 → lastYear = 2047)
- **Structural gap years** (currently 2037-2039) are the consecutive block of years immediately before 2040 where no TIPS have been issued. Derived by walking backward from 2039 until a year with TIPS is found in market data. Years outside this structural block (2036 and below) that have no current holdings are treated as funded-year rungs needing addition, not gap years. Structural gap years do NOT break contiguity if 2040 is held — **mechanism**: if 2040 is held, the range unconditionally extends through the gap years.
- **Non-contiguous TIPS > 2040**: Contribute annual interest to earlier years but are NOT counted as rungs for DARA calculation or ladder range determination unless `lastYearOverride` extends the range to include them.
- **`lastYearOverride`** (optional UI parameter): When provided, replaces the derived `lastYear`. Allows the user to declare the intended last funded year, which may be a gap year (e.g., 2039) or an extended year (e.g., 2042). The UI infers a sensible default and shows a dropdown for manual adjustment.
  - **Shortening**: When `lastYearOverride < derivedLastYear`, all holdings in years `(lastYearOverride, derivedLastYear]` that were contiguous with the original ladder are sold in full (produce sell rows in output). These years are excluded from the rebuild.
  - **Extending**: When `lastYearOverride > derivedLastYear`, years in `(derivedLastYear, lastYearOverride]` with no current holdings are treated as rebalance years — the appropriate TIPS for those years are identified from market data and buy rows are generated.
- **`firstYear` UI inference**: When a Format 4/5 CSV is loaded, if `derivedFirstYear` is a pre-gap Jan TIPS year (currently 2036) and all its holdings have `excessQty = qty` (pure bracket, no funded component), the UI infers the true `firstYear` using: `numGapYears = round(totalExcessPI / roughDARA)` → `firstYear = 2040 − numGapYears`. `totalExcessPI = excessQty(2036) × piPerBond(2036) + excessQty(2040) × piPerBond(2040)`. `roughDARA` = median ARA of non-bracket, non-gap funded years. This works because bracket excess P+I ≈ `numGapYears × DARA` (spec §Gap Year Coverage Model). The inferred gap year becomes the default dropdown selection. For Format 1/2/3 CSVs (no `excessQty`), no inference is attempted and `firstYear` defaults to `derivedFirstYear`.
- **`firstYearOverride`** (optional UI parameter): When provided, replaces the derived `firstYear` (earliest year with holdings). Holdings in years `[derivedFirstYear, firstYearOverride)` are sold in full (produce sell rows in output). These years are excluded from the rebuild.
  - **firstYear as a gap year**: When `firstYearOverride ∈ {2037, 2038, 2039}` (gap years), the ladder starts inside the structural gap block. No holdings exist below the gap, but the structural lower bracket (nearest pre-gap Jan TIPS, currently Jan 2036) is still identified from `tipsMap`. `identifyBrackets` falls back to `tipsMap` when the holdings search yields no lower bracket, setting `lowerCUSIP`, `lowerYear`, and `lowerMaturity` from `tipsMap`. Standard 2-bracket duration-matching weights apply. In full rebalance, a BUY order for 2036 excess is generated (`qtyBefore = 0`, `excessQtyAfter = ROUND(totalBlockCost × lowerWeight / costPerBond_2036)`). Build: the 2036 bond is inserted into `yearBondMap`/`rangeYears` before the sweep with `fundedYearQty = 0` (pure bracket excess). The bracket identification step excludes holdings from years < `firstYear` (being sold) so they don't incorrectly satisfy the lower-bracket search.
  - **3-bracket with firstYear inside the gap**: When `bracketMode = '3bracket'` and `firstYear ∈ {2037, 2038, 2039}`, `minGapYear = firstYear` and `minGapYear − 1` is itself a gap year with no Jan TIPS. The "new lower" search must walk `tipsMap` for the highest Jan year strictly below `minGapYear` (same logic as `anchorBefore`) rather than checking year `minGapYear − 1` exactly. This yields Jan 2036, which is also the `brackets.lowerYear` from `identifyBrackets`. Since `newLowerYear === brackets.lowerYear`, the 3-bracket mode auto-degrades to standard 2-bracket — correct behavior, no error.

### lastYear as a Gap Year

When `lastYearOverride` is a gap year (i.e., `lastYear ∈ gapYears`), the upper bracket year (2040) is **not a funded rung** — all its holdings are treated as bracket excess:

- `fundedYearQty(2040) = 0` in Phase 3 and Phase 4 (`yearDara(2040) = 0`)
- `excessQtyBefore(2040)` follows the standard cost formula (`ROUND(totalBlockCost × weight_2040 / costPerBond_2040)`). Since `fyQtyAfter(2040) = 0`, `fyQtyBefore = qtyBefore − excessQtyBefore` approaches zero when the portfolio was correctly built.
- Phase 2 (`calculateGapParameters`): 2040's LMI contribution uses **actual current holdings qty** (not a target computed from DARA) since no funded-year target exists for 2040.
- `rungCount` = `lastYear − firstYear + 1` (gap years count as rungs; their income is funded by bracket excess)

**UI inference for default lastYear**: After loading holdings, if the last maturity is 2040, `lastYear` defaults to 2040 (or the last gap year, 2039, if the gap ends immediately before). If the last maturity is before 2040, that year is used. The dropdown includes all available TIPS years to allow extension.

### RefCPI Date Override

By default, RefCPI is looked up for the settlement date. The UI allows overriding the reference date (click the RefCPI value in the info strip). When overridden:
- All P+I, cost, and ARA calculations use the RefCPI for the selected date instead of the settlement date
- Auto-inferred DARA (if previously computed) is cleared — rerun to refresh
- The info strip shows the override date in grey next to the RefCPI value

### DARA

#### Per-Year DARA from Portfolio (File Load)

When a holdings file is loaded in Rebalance mode, the UI automatically computes the current Annual Real Amount (ARA) for each funded year from the portfolio and populates the per-year DARA panel:

1. **Raw ARA per year**: process holdings longest-to-shortest, accumulating LMI (annual coupon from later bonds). `ARA_year = Σ(qty × piPerBond) + LMI_from_later`.
2. **Bracket detection**: compute the median ARA across all funded years. Any year with raw ARA > 1.5 × median is a bracket year — its displayed DARA target is substituted with the median to avoid inflating the funded-year target with excess bracket bond P+I.
3. **Scalar DARA field**: set to the median (rounded), serving as the fallback for any year not explicitly in the per-year store.
4. **Panel auto-opens** so the user can review or modify individual years before running.

Using these per-year ARA values as DARA targets produces **no changes** on the first Run Rebalance (the portfolio is already at those targets). To rebuild at a new uniform target, type a new scalar in the DARA field (overrides all years). To adjust individual years, edit the per-year panel directly.

#### Scalar DARA Field

The main DARA field is the fallback for funded years that have no per-year entry. If the user types a new scalar value, it applies to all years not overridden in the per-year panel. If the field is blank, the UI uses `_daraByYearPanelDefault` (set at file load from the portfolio median).

#### Re-run Behaviour

On every Run Rebalance click, `_daraByYearPanelDefault` is synced to `finalDara` before `getDaraByYear()` is called, ensuring stored per-year values are applied correctly regardless of whether the panel is visible.

### Pre-Ladder Interest (PLI) in Rebalance

When `preLadderInterest = true`, the pre-ladder pool, its zeroing, and its partial-credit
distribution are computed by the **shared `sizeLadder`** (ladder-core.js) — the *same* function build
calls — not by a rebalance-local copy. Rebalance derives the canonical bond set with
`selectLadderBonds(firstYear, lastYear)`, runs `sizeLadder`, and consumes its
`zeroedFundedYears`, `partialCreditYear`, `partialCredit`, `pliCreditByGapYear`, and
`pliCreditByFundedYear`. The pre-ladder pool is sized from a canonical (DARA-based) prelim sweep over
`[firstYear, lastYear]`; `preLadderPool = preLadderYears × totalAnnualInt + preLadderAmdPool`,
distributed short→long with gap years consuming credit before longer funded years.

**Why this matters (history)**: rebalance previously hand-wrote its own prelim sweep + pool, which
drifted from build's at the partial-credit boundary year (2040) — build credited ~$11k there,
rebalance ~$4.3k, yielding a 4-bond / ~$6k discrepancy on a build→rebalance round-trip. Sourcing the
PLI distribution from `sizeLadder` makes the round-trip produce **zero trades / zero net cash**.

### Build ≡ Rebalance "After" (unification invariant)

The rebalance **"Amount After"** for every funded year must equal **build's "Amount"** for that year,
and **every** build funded year must render in the rebalance table. This is the contract that makes a
build → export → import → rebalance round-trip exact, and it is locked by tests/run.js
("rebal After == build amount per year", worst $0 across flat / +PLI / variable / variable+PLI). Two
mechanisms enforce it:

1. **One shared Amount rule — `ladder-core.fundedYearAmount`.** The per-year Amount (and its
   pre-ladder credit) is computed by a *single* exported function that **both** `build-lib.js` and the
   rebalance "After" sweep call — there is no second copy of the formula to drift. It returns
   `amount = principal + ownCoupon + laterMatInt + ownExcessCoupon + AMD + credit`, where for a fully
   PLI-funded (zeroed) year `credit = max(0, DARA_y − fixedIncome)` (reconciled so the row lands exactly
   on its DARA) and otherwise `credit = partialCredit` (the year's non-zeroed pre-ladder share, else 0).
   This matters because `sizeLadder` sizes the PLI pool against the *preliminary* later-mat interest
   (the approximation that decides *which* years zero), so `pliCreditByFundedYear[y]` is a prelim-basis
   credit, whereas a row's ARA is built from the *corrected* components (`lAfter` + own-year excess
   coupon `exIntA` + AMD); the shared rule reconciles the zeroed-year credit to those corrected
   components. Without it a zeroed year overshot its DARA by `(lAfter − prelimLMI)` (e.g. 2034 showing
   $22,957 against a $20,000 DARA under variable DARA + PLI). A *non-zeroed* year that later-maturity
   interest alone over-covers legitimately exceeds its DARA (e.g. 2035 → $31,790 vs a $30,000 DARA) —
   **build shows the same value**; that is not an overshoot, the rung simply needs no bonds.
   Display-only: trades use the sized quantities.

2. **Render fully-covered rungs.** Rebalance emits "After" rows from holdings + buys, so a ladder rung
   that is fully covered (zeroed by PLI, or over-covered by LMI) — hence has no holding *and* no buy —
   would silently vanish (the "skipped 2035" bug). After the holdings/buy rows, a final pass over the
   canonical `rangeYears` emits any missing rung as an informational row (`qtyBefore = qtyAfter = 0`,
   Amount After = `postARAByYear[y]`), mirroring build, which always renders every ladder year.

**AMD-inclusive DARA inference**: when `dara = null`, the inferred DARA is the median of each
funded year's `P+I + LMI + held-2052 AMD`. Because Option C credits held-to-maturity 2052 AMD to
every funded year's DARA, the raw `P+I + LMI` median understates DARA by ~one year's AMD; adding the
AMD the portfolio actually earns (scaled by held 2052 excess) recovers the true DARA.

### Per-Year DARA (`daraByYear`)

An optional `Map<year, number>` allowing any ladder year — funded, gap, or Future 30Y — to target a different annual real amount. When provided:

- Each funded year in Phase 4 uses `daraByYear.get(year) ?? DARA` as its target — years absent from the map fall back to the scalar DARA
- Gap years use `daraByYear.get(year) ?? DARA` in the synthetic-qty formula and in the PLI-pool need estimate; a higher gap-year DARA increases synthetic gap quantities, which raises `gapParams.totalCost` and therefore increases bracket excess quantities
- Future 30Y years use `daraByYear.get(year) ?? DARA` in the hypothetical-qty formula; a higher future-30Y DARA increases `future30yTotalCost` and therefore increases future 30Y cover excess quantities
- Bracket target funded-year qty is computed with the bracket year's own DARA from the map
- When `daraByYear` is null/absent, behaviour is identical to the scalar-DARA path

## Named Quantities & Formulas (Rebalance)

**Funded-Year Quantity Before** (`fyQtyBefore`):
```
fyQtyBefore_b = ROUND((DARA − LMIFromAbove_Before − nonTargetPI) / piPerBond_b)
```
`LMIFromAbove_Before` = sum of annual interest from all actual holdings in years maturing after year b (includes both funded and excess holdings at those years). `nonTargetPI` = P+I from non-target CUSIPs held in the same bracket year. Future30y cover years are excluded — treated as pure excess (`fyQtyBefore = 0`). This formula is applied to ALL standard gap bracket years regardless of CSV format.

**Excess Quantity Before** (`excessQtyBefore`):
```
excessQtyBefore_b = MAX(0, qtyBefore_b − fyQtyBefore_b)
```
Funded-first rule: apply the funded-year claim first; the remainder of the held quantity is the current excess. This *derives* the before-split rather than reading the import's excess column back verbatim — but the **imported excess is not discarded**. It still does its two jobs: in duration matching it is the cover/bracket excess (the recorded build intent), and in funded sizing its annual interest and realized AMD role-play as later-maturity interest (`LMIFromAbove_Before` above counts both funded **and** excess holdings). The *size* of the imported cover excess is also what recovers the intended first and last funded year (see 2.0 §Gap Year Coverage Model and §Future 30Y Rungs). Because a correctly-built ladder's derived split reproduces its own imported excess, a build→export→import→rebalance round-trip yields no trades. For Future-30Y cover years: `excessQtyBefore = ROUND(future30yTotalCost × weight_b / costPerBond_b)` (cover-pair cost split).

**Excess Quantity After** (`excessQtyAfter`):
```
excessQtyAfter_b = ROUND(totalBlockCost × weight_b / costPerBond_b)
```
Post-rebalance excess for duration matching. `costPerBond = price/100 × indexRatio × 1000` (per **TIPS Basics**).

**3-bracket lower bracket priority rule** (replaces the weight-based formula for orig lower and new lower):

`targetLowerCost = totalBlockCost × bracketWeights(dur_newLower, dur_upper, dGap).lowerWeight`
(2-bracket formula using only the new lower / Jan 2036 duration)

`totalCurrentLowerCost = excessQtyBefore_origLower × costPerBond_origLower + excessQtyBefore_newLower × costPerBond_newLower`

If `totalCurrentLowerCost > targetLowerCost` (**over-allocated** — Scenario 1):
- Sell orig lower first: `olTargetCost = max(0, origLowerExcessCost − min(origLowerExcessCost, overage))`
- Sell new lower only if overage remains after depleting orig lower: `nlTargetCost = max(0, newLowerExcessCost − remainingOverage)`

If `totalCurrentLowerCost ≤ targetLowerCost` (**under-allocated** — Scenario 2):
- Freeze orig lower at current: `olTargetCost = origLowerExcessCost` (never increased)
- Buy new lower to fill: `nlTargetCost = max(0, targetLowerCost − origLowerExcessCost)`

`excessQtyAfter_origLower = ROUND(olTargetCost / costPerBond_origLower)`
`excessQtyAfter_newLower  = ROUND(nlTargetCost / costPerBond_newLower)`

**Cash Delta** (`cashDelta`, `costDelta`):
```
cashDelta = -(qtyDelta × costPerTips)
  where qtyDelta = qtyAfter - qtyBefore
```
Positive = cash received (net sell); negative = cash outflow (net buy).

## Bracket Identification Rules

Rules differ by bracket mode:

### 2-Bracket Lower Bracket (Canonical)
The lower bracket is **always** the latest Jan TIPS from `tipsMap` with maturity year strictly below `minGapYear` (currently Jan 2036). Holdings are not consulted. This is the same search used for `anchorBefore` in gap parameter calculation. Any older holdings that previously served as the lower bracket (e.g. Jan 2034) are no longer in `bracketYearSet`; Full rebalance rebuilds them to funded-year qty only, selling any excess.

### 3-Bracket Original Lower Bracket (Holdings-Based)
For the original lower bracket only, the identification is holdings-based:
1.  **CUSIP Aggregation**: Sum total quantity of each CUSIP across the entire portfolio.
2.  **Excess ARA Priority**: The primary metric is **Excess ARA** (`araByYear[y] - DARA`). The year with the greatest positive difference is the original lower bracket. **Note**: "Excess ARA" is a one-time empirical probe used solely here — not a general concept.
3.  **Tie-Breaking**: Among candidates with equal Excess ARA, pick the CUSIP with the highest aggregate quantity.
4.  **Range**: Only years in `[LOWEST_LOWER_BRACKET_YEAR (2032), minGapYear)` are considered.
5.  **Fallback**: If no holdings-based lower bracket is found (e.g. `firstYear` inside the gap), `identifyBrackets` falls back to the tipsMap canonical search — same result as 2-bracket.

## Algorithm Flow (Gap-Only)

### Phase 1: Derive Parameters
- Settlement date, RefCPI, DARA.
- Build holdings ladder and identify official range.
- Calculate **inferred DARA** from current ARA.
- Identify current gap years.

### Phase 2: Missing Block Parameters
- Calculate **Gap Avg Duration** and **Gap Total Cost** (see **2.0 TIPS Ladders**).
- If `lastYear > 2056`, calculate **Future 30Y Avg Duration** and **Future 30Y Total Cost**.

### Phase 3: Bracket Resolution
- Identify lower/upper bracket CUSIPs from holdings per §Bracket Identification Rules.
- Calculate modified duration for each bracket.
- Solve weights using **2.0 TIPS Ladders** (2-bracket or 3-bracket mode).
- If `lastYear > 2056`, identify Future 30Y cover pair and solve weights.

### Phase 4: Ladder Rebuild (longest to shortest)
Process ALL years in [firstYear, lastYear] **longest to shortest**.
- **Bracket years**: use BEFORE-state LMI pool to ensure `fyQty` remains constant.
- **Rebalance years**: use running post-rebalance LMI pool.
- Apply bracket excess quantities on top of target `fyQty`.
- **LMI pool rule**: Process according to **2.0 TIPS Ladders §Algorithm**. Excess bracket holdings are actual bonds — their interest reduces funded-year quantities of all shorter rungs, on equal footing with funded-year holdings.

## Excess Balance Check

Validates historical funding of the gap coverage task:
```
1. Previous excess cost
2. Cost to fill rebalance years
3. Gap theoretical cost

Surplus/(Shortfall) = 1 - 2 - 3
```
Near-zero indicates brackets were properly funded. Large negative indicates brackets were underfunded relative to the current DARA target.
