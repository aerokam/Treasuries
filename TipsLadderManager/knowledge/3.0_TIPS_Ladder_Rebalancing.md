## Purpose

Rebalance an existing TIPS ladder to align it with its desired-amount targets (DARA), **which may vary year to year** (see 2.0 §Duration Matching — per-year DARA may be uneven). There is a **single rebalance**: it rebuilds the ladder from the current portfolio toward the per-year DARA targets, buying and selling TIPS as required. (An earlier "Gap" mode that did minimal trades was scrapped; the `method` parameter, the `#method` UI input, and the `isFullMode`/`!isFullMode` branches have been removed. `runRebalance` no longer takes a `method`.)

## Context

TIPS ladders require periodic rebalancing as new TIPS are issued — filling former gap years so their coverage can move from bracket excess into the year's own rung — and as market prices drift the income profile. A rebalance realigns the ladder to its per-year DARA targets using the current portfolio.

## Rebalancing-Specific Concepts

### Official Ladder Range

- **firstYear**: Earliest year with holdings
- **lastYear**: Latest contiguous year with holdings before any missing rung above 2040.
- Missing rungs above 2040 truncate the official range (e.g., holdings in 2047 and 2049 but not 2048 → lastYear = 2047)
- **Structural gap years** (currently 2037-2039) are the consecutive block of years immediately before 2040 where no TIPS have been issued. Derived by walking backward from 2039 until a year with TIPS is found in market data. Years outside this structural block (2036 and below) that have no current holdings are treated as funded-year rungs needing addition, not gap years. Structural gap years do NOT break contiguity if 2040 is held — **mechanism**: if 2040 is held, the range unconditionally extends through the gap years.
- **Non-contiguous TIPS > 2040**: Contribute annual interest to earlier years but are NOT counted as rungs for DARA calculation or ladder range determination unless `lastYearOverride` extends the range to include them.
- **`lastYearOverride`** (optional UI parameter): When provided, replaces the derived `lastYear`. Allows the user to declare the intended last funded year, which may be a gap year (e.g., 2039) or an extended year (e.g., 2042). The UI infers a sensible default and shows a dropdown for manual adjustment.
  - **Shortening**: When `lastYearOverride < derivedLastYear`, all holdings in years `(lastYearOverride, derivedLastYear]` that were contiguous with the original ladder are sold in full (produce sell rows in output). These years are excluded from the rebuild.
  - **Extending**: When `lastYearOverride > derivedLastYear`, years in `(derivedLastYear, lastYearOverride]` with no current holdings are treated as rebalance years — the appropriate TIPS for those years are identified from market data and buy rows are generated.
- **`firstYear` UI inference**: When a Format 4/5 CSV is loaded, if `derivedFirstYear` is a pre-gap Jan TIPS year (currently 2036) and all its holdings have `excessQty = qty` (pure bracket, no funded component), the UI infers the true `firstYear` using: `numGapYears = round(totalExcessPI / roughDARA)` → `firstYear = 2040 − numGapYears`. `totalExcessPI = excessQty(2036) × piPerBond(2036) + excessQty(2040) × piPerBond(2040)`. `roughDARA` = median ARA of non-bracket, non-gap funded years. This works because bracket excess P+I ≈ `numGapYears × DARA` (spec §Gap Year Coverage Model). The inferred gap year becomes the default dropdown selection. For Format 1/2/3 CSVs (no `excessQty`), no inference is attempted and `firstYear` defaults to `derivedFirstYear`.
- **`firstYearOverride`** (optional UI parameter): When provided, replaces the derived `firstYear` (earliest year with holdings). Holdings in years `[derivedFirstYear, firstYearOverride)` are sold in full (produce sell rows in output). These years are excluded from the rebuild.
  - **firstYear as a gap year**: When `firstYearOverride ∈ {2037, 2038, 2039}` (gap years), the ladder starts inside the structural gap block. No holdings exist below the gap, but the structural lower bracket (nearest pre-gap Jan TIPS, currently Jan 2036) is still identified from `tipsMap`. `identifyBrackets` falls back to `tipsMap` when the holdings search yields no lower bracket, setting `lowerCUSIP`, `lowerYear`, and `lowerMaturity` from `tipsMap`. Standard 2-bracket duration-matching weights apply. In full rebalance, a BUY order for 2036 excess is generated (`qtyBefore = 0`, `excessQtyAfter = ROUND(totalBlockCost × lowerWeight / costPerBond_2036)`). Build: the 2036 bond is inserted into `yearBondMap`/`rangeYears` before the sweep with `fundedYearQty = 0` (pure bracket excess). The bracket identification step excludes holdings from years < `firstYear` (being sold) so they don't incorrectly satisfy the lower-bracket search.
  - **3-bracket with firstYear inside the gap**: When `bracketMode = '3bracket'` and `firstYear ∈ {2037, 2038, 2039}`, `minGapYear = firstYear` and `minGapYear − 1` is itself a gap year with no Jan TIPS. The "new lower" search must walk `tipsMap` for the highest Jan year strictly below `minGapYear` (same logic as `anchorBefore`) rather than checking year `minGapYear − 1` exactly. This yields Jan 2036, which is also the `brackets.lowerYear` from `identifyBrackets`. Since `newLowerYear === brackets.lowerYear`, the 3-bracket mode auto-degrades to standard 2-bracket — correct behavior, no error.

### lastYear as a Gap Year

When `lastYearOverride` is a gap year (i.e., `lastYear ∈ gapYears`), the upper bracket year (2040) is **not a funded rung** — all its holdings are treated as bracket excess:

- `fundedYearQty(2040) = 0` in Phase 3 and Phase 4 (`yearDara(2040) = 0`)
- `excessQtyBefore(2040)` follows the standard cost formula (`ROUND(totalBlockCost × weight_2040 / costPerBond_2040)`). Since `fyQtyAfter(2040) = 0`, `fyQtyBefore = qtyBefore − excessQtyBefore` approaches zero when the portfolio was correctly built.
- Phase 2 (`calculateGapParameters`): 2040's LMI contribution uses **actual current holdings qty** (not a target computed from DARA) since no funded-year target exists for 2040.
- `rungCount` = `lastYear − firstYear + 1` (gap years count as rungs; their income is funded by bracket excess)

**UI inference for default lastYear**: After loading holdings, if the last maturity is 2040, `lastYear` defaults to 2040 (or the last gap year, 2039, if the gap ends immediately before). If the last maturity is before 2040, that year is used. The dropdown includes all available TIPS years to allow extension.

### RefCPI Date Override

By default, RefCPI is looked up for the settlement date. The UI allows overriding the reference date (click the RefCPI value in the info strip). When overridden:
- All P+I, cost, and ARA calculations use the RefCPI for the selected date instead of the settlement date
- The per-year DARA mirror (computed from holdings at load) is cleared — rerun to refresh
- The info strip shows the override date in grey next to the RefCPI value

**Date picker input**: the shared native date picker (`shared/src/date-picker.js`, class `.date-picker`) — the browser's native calendar/keyboard entry, value in ISO `YYYY-MM-DD`. It sits inside the override popup; pressing Enter or clicking Apply commits the date; Reset reverts to the default settlement date.

**Ref CPI interpolation popup** (level-3 drill from any Ref CPI row): shows the active ref CPI date (not today's date) as "Settlement Date", and displays the m-3 and m-2 CPI-U values that correspond to that date. The date passed to `buildRefCpiDrill` is always `activeRefCpiDateStr ?? _defaultRefCpiDateStr`, formatted mm/dd/yyyy.

### DARA

#### Per-Year DARA from Portfolio (File Load) — mirror current holdings, **no inference**

When a holdings file **without an explicit `#fundedYear,dara` block** (broker, legacy Format-5, tipsladder Format-4) is loaded in Rebalance mode, the per-year DARA panel is populated to **mirror the current portfolio** — the Annual Real Amount (ARA) each rung already throws off. **No DARA is inferred, scaled, or self-finance-solved at load.** (Our own exports carry the `#fundedYear,dara` block and take the exact-reproduction path instead — see 2.1 Broker Import.)

1. **Raw ARA, every in-range year**: process holdings longest-to-shortest, accumulating LMI (annual coupon from later bonds). For a **held** year `ARA_year = Σ(qty × piPerBond) + LMI_from_later`; for an ordinary **empty** in-range year (TIPS exist for that maturity, the user simply holds none) `ARA_year = LMI_from_later` alone. Every year in `[firstYear, lastYear]` is written to the per-year store — empty years included — so the panel shows each rung's true current income. An empty year between two holdings shows just the coupon dripping down from the later bond (e.g. ~$230), **not** a full rung.
2. **Gap years use the funded-year median**: a structural **gap year** (2037–2039 — no TIPS issued for anyone, identified by `getGapYears(tipsMap)`) cannot be held directly, so there is no holding to read a DARA from. The bare LMI dripping down would understate the rung (it is funded by bracket excess at the adjacent years, not by a bond of its own). Each gap year with no supplied DARA therefore falls back to the **funded-year median** (step 3). *(Future-30Y years 2057+ are out of scope for basic files — they only appear via our own export, which carries an explicit `#fundedYear,dara` block.)*
3. **Bracket cap (held years only)**: the median is taken over the **held** funded years; empty LMI-only years are excluded so they cannot collapse the median. Any gap-adjacent bracket-candidate year whose raw ARA > 1.5 × that median is capped to the median, to avoid inflating its target with excess bracket-bond P+I.
4. **Scalar DARA field**: set to the held-year median (rounded) — the representative rung size, used as the fallback only when the user later clears the per-year store and types a single scalar.
5. **Panel auto-opens** so the user can review or modify individual years before running.

This per-year store is the **shape** the rebalance preserves. At **load** nothing is scaled or solved — the panel shows the portfolio as-is. The self-financing adjustment happens at **Run** (next subsection). The user may also **explicitly** reshape before running: type a new scalar in the DARA field, edit individual years, or use the segment **Infer** buttons (see §Two-Segment DARA). Nothing is inferred on the user's behalf at load.

#### Funding the rebalance — shape-preserving self-financing scale (Run)

A rebalance must be **self-financing**: net cash (`costDeltaSum`) is a small **non-negative** number — the proceeds of what is sold cover what is bought, with no external cash added. The duration-match bracket excess (sized from the gap block's total cost, see 2.0 §Excess Quantities) is the purchase that must be funded; the cash to buy it is raised by **selling proportionally across the funded rungs**, scaling the entire per-year shape down by a single factor so the ladder keeps its (possibly uneven) shape.

This is **one coupled solve, not a conditional step**, because the inputs are interdependent: the gap-year DARA is the funded-year median, the gap block's total cost depends on that median, the excess to buy depends on the cost, and the cash raised (hence the scale factor) depends on the excess. A **binary search on a single DARA level** dissolves the loop: at each trial level `L` the whole shape is rebuilt at `k = L / median` (every rung × `k`; gap years track the scaled median), the rebalance is run, and `L` is driven to the **highest value at which `costDeltaSum ≥ 0`**. The result is the self-financing scaled per-year map fed to the executed rebalance.

- **Implementation:** `inferScaledDARAFromPortfolio` with `flat = false` (shape-preserving). This is the **single source** for the solve — the same function the segment **Infer** buttons call (they use `flat = true`, a flat shape; Run uses `flat = false`, the natural shape). Wired into the standard Run path; the displayed panel is **not** rewritten (the user keeps seeing the mirrored/edited shape).
- **The scale is applied ONLY when there is a gap-year / Future-30Y block to fund.** The scale exists solely to raise cash for the duration-match excess those blocks require. Run first executes the rebalance on the per-year store directly; **only if the engine reports gap years or Future-30Y years in range** (`summary.gapYears` / `summary.future30yYears` non-empty) **and** the store is the untouched load mirror is the shape-preserving scale applied (re-run with the scaled map). With no such block there is nothing to buy, so the direct run already nets to ≈ 0 and **no scaling is done** — the held rungs are untouched. (Gating off the engine's own summary avoids re-deriving the ladder range in the UI.) This whole direct-run → conditional-scale orchestration is the library export **`runFundedRebalance`** (single home, unit-tested); the UI shell just passes the per-year store, the `isPristineMirror` flag, and the form values.
  - **Why the gate is necessary (not just an optimization):** the scaled solve recovers its shape from the **held** funded years only; ordinary **empty interior years** (a maturity the user simply holds none of — *not* a structural gap year) are absent from that map and would fall back to the **scalar** DARA inside `runRebalance`, which sizes them as full rungs and emits spurious BUYs. The self-financing search then reads those phantom buys as "excess to fund" and sells the entire ladder down to raise the cash — large, wrong trades on a portfolio that needed none. An **empty interior funded year is an intentional hole** (the user holds none on purpose); the rebalance **never auto-fills it**. The load mirror already encodes this correctly (the empty year carries its incoming LMI, "not a full rung" — see §Per-Year DARA from Portfolio step 1), so running the store directly is a true no-op.

#### Intentional empty rungs and the rung-validation waiver

An **empty interior funded year is an intentional hole by default** — but it is a *default*, not a hard floor. The rule is per-year and provenance-free: the year's **own per-year DARA** decides.

- **Untouched (mirror) empty year** — its DARA is its incoming-LMI stub, so `round((DARA − LMI) / piPerBond)` lands at **0**: a hole, no trade. This is the no-op.
- **Explicitly raised empty year** — when the user lifts that year's DARA above its incoming LMI by at least one bond's worth (e.g. **Infer LMP** stamps a flat segment DARA across *every* LMP rung including the empty ones, or a manual edit), the year sizes to **≥ 1 bond and fills normally**. The panel value is the user's stated intent and is honored — there is no separate "is this a hole" flag.

Because of the first case, `sizeLadder`'s "every funded year must fund at least one bond" validation (which throws `daraTooLowYear`) is **waived for the rebalance's unheld in-range years** (`optionalYears`): an empty year whose target rounds to 0 is a hole, not a too-low-DARA error. The waiver only matters at the knife-edge (an empty year's mirror DARA ≈ its own incoming LMI, which can round either side of the prelim-LMI estimate); a year the user genuinely funds has target ≥ 1 and never reaches the throw. Build passes no `optionalYears` — every build year is a required rung, so its too-low-DARA error stands.
- **Scope — default mirror only.** The Run-time scale applies when the per-year store is the **as-loaded mirror** (unmodified). Once the user has explicitly reshaped (per-rung edits, segment **Infer**, or segment constant), that shape is the user's stated intent and is honored **as-is** — segment Infer already self-finances each segment (see §Two-Segment DARA). Files carrying an explicit `#fundedYear,dara` block (our own exports) are likewise honored exactly — **no Run-time scale** — so the build→export→import round-trip stays zero-trade (see 2.1 Broker Import).
- **Relationship to 2.0:** this rule sits entirely above 2.0. 2.0 §Algorithm *sizes* each rung from its DARA and credits bracket-excess coupon down as LMI; it says nothing about cash balancing. The self-financing scale chooses the one DARA level at which 2.0's sizing nets to non-negative cash. No 2.0 claim is altered.

#### Scalar DARA Field

The main DARA field is the fallback for funded years that have no per-year entry. If the user types a new scalar value, it applies to all years not overridden in the per-year panel. If the field is blank, the UI uses `_daraByYearPanelDefault` (set at file load from the portfolio median).

#### Re-run Behaviour

On every Run Rebalance click, `_daraByYearPanelDefault` is synced to `finalDara` before `getDaraByYear()` is called, ensuring stored per-year values are applied correctly regardless of whether the panel is visible.

### Pre-Ladder Interest (PLI) in Rebalance

When `preLadderInterest = true`, the pre-ladder pool, its zeroing, and its partial-credit
distribution are computed by the **shared `sizeLadder`** (ladder-core.js) — the *same* function build
calls — not by a rebalance-local copy. Rebalance derives the canonical bond set with
`selectLadderBonds(firstYear, lastYear)`, runs `sizeLadder`, and consumes its
`zeroedFundedYears`, `partialCreditYear`, `partialCredit`, `pliCreditByGapYear`, and
`pliCreditByFundedYear`. The pre-ladder pool is sized from a canonical (DARA-based) prelim sweep over
`[firstYear, lastYear]`; `preLadderPool = preLadderYears × totalAnnualInt + preLadderAmdPool`,
distributed short→long with gap years consuming credit before longer funded years.

**Why this matters (history)**: rebalance previously hand-wrote its own prelim sweep + pool, which
drifted from build's at the partial-credit boundary year (2040) — build credited ~$11k there,
rebalance ~$4.3k, yielding a 4-bond / ~$6k discrepancy on a build→rebalance round-trip. Sourcing the
PLI distribution from `sizeLadder` makes the round-trip produce **zero trades / zero net cash**.

### Build ≡ Rebalance "Before" and "After" (unification invariant)

The rebalance **"Amount After"** for every funded year must equal **build's "Amount"** for that year,
and — for a no-trade round-trip of *our own* file — the **"Amount Before"** must equal it too (the held
ladder IS the target). **Every** build funded year must render in the rebalance table. This is the
contract that makes a build → export → import → rebalance round-trip exact, and it is locked by
tests/run.js ("rebal After == build amount per year" **and** "rebal Before == build amount per year",
worst $0 across flat / +PLI / variable / variable+PLI). Three mechanisms enforce it:

1. **One shared Amount rule — `ladder-core.fundedYearAmount`.** The per-year Amount (and its
   pre-ladder credit) is computed by a *single* exported function that `build-lib.js` **and both** the
   rebalance "Before" and "After" sweeps call — there is no second copy of the formula to drift. It returns
   `amount = principal + ownCoupon + laterMatInt + ownExcessCoupon + AMD + rollCoupon + credit` (where
   `rollCoupon` is the Future-30Y cover-roll coupon credited to 2053–56; see 2.0 §Cover-roll coupon), where for a fully
   PLI-funded (zeroed) year `credit = max(0, DARA_y − fixedIncome)` (reconciled so the row lands exactly
   on its DARA) and otherwise `credit = partialCredit` (the year's non-zeroed pre-ladder share, else 0).
   This matters because `sizeLadder` sizes the PLI pool against the *preliminary* later-mat interest
   (the approximation that decides *which* years zero), so `pliCreditByFundedYear[y]` is a prelim-basis
   credit, whereas a row's ARA is built from the *corrected* components (`lAfter` + own-year excess
   coupon `exIntA` + AMD); the shared rule reconciles the zeroed-year credit to those corrected
   components. Without it a zeroed year overshot its DARA by `(lAfter − prelimLMI)` (e.g. 2034 showing
   $22,957 against a $20,000 DARA under variable DARA + PLI). A *non-zeroed* year that later-maturity
   interest alone over-covers legitimately exceeds its DARA (e.g. 2035 → $31,790 vs a $30,000 DARA) —
   **build shows the same value**; that is not an overshoot, the rung simply needs no bonds.
   Display-only: trades use the sized quantities.

2. **Render fully-covered rungs.** Rebalance emits "After" rows from holdings + buys, so a ladder rung
   that is fully covered (zeroed by PLI, or over-covered by LMI) — hence has no holding *and* no buy —
   would silently vanish (the "skipped 2035" bug). After the holdings/buy rows, a final pass over the
   canonical `rangeYears` emits any missing rung as an informational row (`qtyBefore = qtyAfter = 0`,
   Amount After = `postARAByYear[y]`), mirroring build, which always renders every ladder year.

3. **"Before" runs the same sweep with held quantities.** "Amount Before" is the current-holdings
   valuation, so it uses *held* qtys — but through the **same** `fundedYearAmount` rule as After, with
   the same component set: own principal + own coupon + LMI + **held excess coupon** (`exIntB`) + AMD +
   credit. Previously `beforeARAByYear` was a separate, cruder sum (`pB + cB + lBefore + amdBefore`)
   that omitted the bracket excess coupon and the pre-ladder credit and over-scaled AMD — so Before
   drifted from build even with zero trades (20k+ deficits at zeroed/bracket years like 2036/2040, and
   ~$500/yr from the AMD basis). The pre-ladder **credit in Before is applied only for our own files**
   (those carrying an explicit per-year DARA block); broker files (inferred DARA) keep their existing
   Before semantics until broker handling is revisited. For a broker file *with* trades, Before ≠ After
   legitimately — that difference is the point of "Before". **Bracket display:** a designated gap
   bracket renders with `*` + a qty-0 excess sub-row even at 0 excess, in **both** build and rebalance
   (`render.js isBracket` uses `excess > 0 || isGapBracket` for both modes) — previously rebalance hid a
   0-excess bracket (e.g. 2036/2040 under high later-year DARA), while build showed it.

**AMD-inclusive DARA inference**: when `dara = null`, the inferred DARA is the median of each
funded year's `P+I + LMI + held-2052 AMD + Future-30Y roll coupon`. Because Option C credits held-to-maturity
2052 AMD (and, for 2053–56, the cover-roll coupon) to every funded year's DARA, the raw `P+I + LMI` median
understates DARA; adding the AMD **and** roll coupon the portfolio actually earns (both scaled by held 2052
excess) recovers the true DARA. The natural-ARA recovery (`inferScaledDARAFromPortfolio`) likewise adds back
`ownExcessCoupon + AMD + rollCoupon` at the cover/post-cover years so the recovered map matches build's identity.

### Per-Year DARA (`daraByYear`)

An optional `Map<year, number>` allowing any ladder year — funded, gap, or Future 30Y — to target a different annual real amount. When provided:

- Each funded year in Phase 4 uses `daraByYear.get(year) ?? DARA` as its target — years absent from the map fall back to the scalar DARA
- Gap years use `daraByYear.get(year) ?? DARA` in the synthetic-qty formula and in the PLI-pool need estimate; a higher gap-year DARA increases synthetic gap quantities, which raises `gapParams.totalCost` and therefore increases bracket excess quantities
- Future 30Y years use `daraByYear.get(year) ?? DARA` in the hypothetical-qty formula; a higher future-30Y DARA increases `future30yTotalCost` and therefore increases future 30Y cover excess quantities
- Bracket target funded-year qty is computed with the bracket year's own DARA from the map
- When `daraByYear` is null/absent, behaviour is identical to the scalar-DARA path

### Segmented DARA (N-way split)

> **Terminology note — LMI+AMD.** "LMI" (later-maturity interest) is now an incomplete name:
> when the ladder extends past 2056 the held excess 2052 TIPS contribute **accrued market
> discount (AMD)** that is credited to earlier years as interest. The income flowing downward
> in the sweep is therefore **LMI+AMD** (later-maturity interest *and* accrued market discount).
> The segment design below depends on this downward flow.

One holdings file can hold **any number** of conceptually distinct ladders the user manages on
**independent DARA targets** — e.g. a near-term liability-matching stretch the user intends to
spend down, then one or more tapering or speculative/heirs stretches beyond it (the motivating
case is a taper: a larger DARA for the first N funded years, smaller amounts over the years after
that, in as many steps as the user wants). Earlier this was fixed at exactly two segments named
"LMP" and "speculative"; that naming doesn't generalize past two, so segments are now anonymous —
labeled only by their own year range — and the two-segment case is just the one-split special case.

The app cannot infer which rungs belong to which segment (both are just TIPS), so the boundaries
are **user input**: any number of **split-year** controls in the Rebalance UI (add/remove via
chips + a dropdown). With no split years the whole ladder is one segment. Split years are
**session-only** for now — structured so they can later persist via `#params` and extend to Build.
(Split years also travel with the exportable DARA-plan file — see 2.1 §Standalone DARA-plan file.)

#### Why a top-down cascade (and not isolated solves)

LMI+AMD flows **only downward** (longer → shorter maturities). The topmost segment sits at the
top of the sweep — nothing above feeds it — while each segment below **receives** LMI+AMD from
every segment above it. Segments are therefore **coupled**: how much a longer segment holds
changes the income dropping into the segments below it, which changes the bonds those lower rungs
need. A literally-isolated solve for a lower segment (ignoring inherited income from above) would
leave the upper segments' coupons unspent, so the **whole-portfolio** run would not net to zero.

The clean decomposition that satisfies *both* "each segment self-finances" *and* "whole-portfolio
net cash ≈ 0" is a **cascade in sweep order, top → bottom, one segment at a time**:

1. **Solve the topmost segment first.** Its rows' cash delta depends only on its own DARA and its
   own holdings (no income from above), so its self-financing level is independent of every
   segment below it. This fixes its target quantities — and thus the LMI+AMD dropping into the
   segments beneath it.
2. **Solve each next segment down** with every segment above it **held fixed**. The inherited
   LMI+AMD is now a fixed boundary credit, so that segment's cash delta depends only on its own
   DARA.
3. Repeat down to the bottommost segment.

Because each segment is driven to its own net-cash ≈ 0, the whole-portfolio net cash (the number
shown in the UI) is the sum ≈ 0. Note this cascade needs no explicit ordered pipeline in the UI:
each segment's Infer solves against whatever is *currently* in `_daraByYearStore` for every other
segment (hand-set or previously inferred), so solving top-to-bottom naturally reproduces the
cascade regardless of how many segments exist (see §UI: segment actions below).

#### Self-finance metric (segment-scoped)

A segment "self-finances" when the cost of rebuilding its target rungs ≈ the liquidation value of
its current holdings — i.e. **segment net cash ≈ 0**, where:

```
segmentNetCash(seg) = Σ cashDelta over rebalance rows whose maturity year ∈ seg
```

This reuses the existing whole-portfolio `runRebalance` unchanged; the solve simply **partitions
the per-row `cashDelta` by maturity year** instead of summing all of them. The cover pair /
Future-30Y excess (held in 2052/2056) is attributed to whichever segment is topmost.

**Trades outside `[firstYear, lastYear]` — attributed to the topmost segment.** When `lastYear`
lands inside the structural gap (2037–2039, see §lastYear as a Gap Year), the upper bracket (2040)
is not a funded rung and sits *above* `lastYear`, but the rebalance still emits a trade for it (its
excess is resized to the gap's actual duration-match need). That trade's `fundedYear` (2040) is
outside every segment's `[firstYear, lastYear]`-bounded `scopeYears`, so a naive scoped sum drops
its `cashDelta` entirely — the search then optimizes a *partial* view of the portfolio and can
converge on a DARA that leaves the true whole-portfolio net cash far from zero (the trade is real:
an oversized bracket position gets sold down, releasing cash no segment accounted for). The fix:
a trade year above a segment's own range is still credited to that segment if its `scopeYears`
reaches `lastYearOverride` (i.e. it's the topmost/last segment) — the same "belongs to whichever
segment reaches the top of the range" rule already used for the Future-30Y cover pair, generalized
to the plain gap's upper bracket. Implemented as `inCashScope` in `inferScaledDARAFromPortfolio`,
kept separate from `inScope` (which still excludes 2040 from `buildMap` — it has no `daraByYear`
target of its own; only its cash delta needs a home).

#### `inferScaledDARAFromPortfolio` — scoped form

The existing function (which scales the whole natural-ARA map until whole-portfolio
`costDeltaSum ≥ 0` is minimal) generalizes to a scoped solve:

- **`scopeYears`** — the segment's years. Only these entries are solved for; years outside the
  scope are taken from **`fixedDaraByYear`** (the other segments' already-decided values, or the
  user's manual entries — the cascade boundary).
- **`flat`** — selects what the in-scope rungs target:
  - **`flat = true` (the segment "Fill median" action):** the binary search finds the **single flat
    DARA** that makes the segment self-finance, and **every in-scope rung is stamped with that one
    value** → even real income. This is what "infer the median, set all rungs to it" means. Note
    this is an **option, not a requirement**: a segment's liabilities may legitimately differ year
    to year (a lumpy ladder), so the user is free to leave it lumpy or edit individual rungs after
    flattening — flattening is simply offered for when even income is what's wanted.
  - **`flat = false` (default):** the natural-ARA *shape* is preserved and scaled by the searched
    factor `k = level/median`, so each rung keeps its own relative income. This is the scoped
    whole-portfolio **infer action** (button-initiated). It is **not** run at file load — file load
    mirrors current holdings with no inference (see §Per-Year DARA from Portfolio). A
    build→export→import round-trip reproduces a variable-DARA shape via the explicit
    `#fundedYear,dara` block (2.1 Broker Import), not via this solve.
- **Self-finance predicate** — drive `segmentNetCash(scopeYears)` (not the whole `costDeltaSum`)
  to minimal ≥ 0.
- The natural-ARA recovery (`computePortfolioARAByYear`) still sees **all** holdings (LMI+AMD needs
  every bond); only the *target* and the *cash-delta sum* are scoped.
- **Infeasible-probe tolerance** — the binary search may probe a DARA that `sizeLadder` rejects:
  driving a segment's target high floods later-maturity interest into a *fixed downstream* segment's
  year until it can't fund one bond, so `sizeLadder` throws (`err.daraTooLowYear`). The search
  catches this — a throw on an **out-of-scope** year means this segment's DARA is too high (search
  lower); a throw on an **in-scope** year means too low (search higher). Without this, a top-segment-only
  infer threw and aborted the handler before re-render (the "Infer speculative does nothing" bug).

`inferSegmentedDARAFromPortfolio` remains a library export (unit-tested) implementing the exact
**2-segment** case of this cascade (`scopeYears = [split+1, last]` then `[first, split]`) as a
cross-check on the general math. The **UI no longer calls it** — for any number of segments, rows
are inferred one at a time in the UI, and the cascade emerges from each Infer solving against
whatever is currently in the store (see below), not from an explicit ordered pipeline.

#### UI: segment actions, ordering, and history

The per-year DARA panel (`_daraByYearStore`) wires these actions. Segments are **asymmetric**:
a higher segment's self-financing DARA is independent of everything below it, while each lower
segment is downstream and self-finances against whatever the segments above it currently hold
(LMI+AMD flows only down). This asymmetry sets the **ordering rule** (infer top to bottom) rather
than forcing an automatic cascade.

**The user is in charge of what gets inferred, and every segment is inferred INDEPENDENTLY.**
Adding or removing a split year only changes where the boundaries fall and reveals the resulting
segment rows (`_renderSegTools`) — **no inference runs on a split-year change**. Inferring or
editing one segment **never rewrites another segment's per-year DARA**.

A higher segment's income still "ripples down" to fund the segments below it, but that is the
**natural LMI+AMD coupon flow inside `runRebalance`** (the longest→shortest sweep), *not* a DARA
rewrite — so it needs no extra wiring and leaves the lower segments' targets alone. (An earlier
build auto-re-inferred the segment below on any change above it; that was removed — it overwrote
targets the user did not want touched.)

Because each segment solves against whatever is **currently** in the segments above it, the
ordering rule for whole-portfolio net cash ≈ 0 is **infer/finalize top to bottom** (longest
maturities first).

- **Segments and split years** — `_splitYears` (array, session-only) partitions
  `[firstYear, lastYear]` via `segmentRanges` (`src/segment-dara.js`) into `_segments()`, an
  ascending array of year-Sets. With `_splitYears = []` there is one segment (the whole ladder).
  Chips + an add-select let the user add/remove split years; each resulting segment renders its
  own row (label = its own year range + an **Infer DARA** button + a **$ each** input), by index
  into `_segments()` — there is no fixed naming (no "LMP"/"speculative" ids) once more than two
  segments exist.
- **Infer DARA (per segment)** (`_runSegmentInfer(idx)`) — scoped `flat = true` solve for that
  segment alone against the current store (`fixedDaraByYear: _daraByYearStore`), so hand-set values
  in every other segment are preserved. Stamps one flat self-financing value across that segment's
  rungs and echoes it in that segment's `$ each` field.
- **Typed constant** (`_runSegmentConst(idx, val)`) — a segment's `$ each` input stamps the typed
  value across that segment's rows on commit (Enter or blur), no separate button — mirroring how
  the main `#dara` field applies on blur. Every other segment is untouched.
- **Manual override** — individual per-year inputs remain editable and win. The scalar `#dara` field
  shows `by year` whenever the store deviates from a single default.
- **Undo blanks the inferred-amount fields** — every segment's `.seg-const-input` is a display-only
  echo of its last infer; `_restoreDara` clears all of them on Undo/Revert since the restored state
  no longer corresponds to those medians.

**Undo / Revert** — every *bulk* mutation (auto-infer, the infer buttons, segment constant, main
`#dara` blur-stamp, the ▲/▼ steppers) pushes a clone of `_daraByYearStore` onto `_daraUndoStack`
first; per-rung edits push one entry per committed edit session (focus-in snapshot, commit on
focus-out if changed). **Undo** (button or Ctrl+Z while the panel is open) pops the stack; **Revert
to loaded** restores `_daraLoadedSnapshot` (the as-imported map, captured in
`_initRebalDaraFromPortfolio`) in one step. Stacks + loaded snapshot are per-mode (swapped with the
store on the rebalance/build tab change) and reset on each new file load.

`_initRebalDaraFromPortfolio` (file load) **mirrors current holdings** — it writes each in-range
year's raw current ARA into the store (empty years = incoming LMI) and infers nothing (see §Per-Year
DARA from Portfolio). All inference is **user-initiated** via the segment Infer buttons and additive
on top of that mirror.

#### Persisting a DARA plan across sessions

Because file load always **mirrors current holdings with no inference** (§Per-Year DARA from
Portfolio), re-uploading a fresh broker export (Vanguard/Fidelity/Schwab) wipes any per-year shape
or split years the user set up on a prior visit — brokers never carry the `#fundedYear,dara` block
that makes *our own* exports reproduce exactly (2.1 Broker Import). Two opt-in mechanisms address
this, neither changing the default mirror-at-load behavior:

- **Opt-in localStorage cache** (`_daraCacheKey` / the "Remember" checkbox) — a same-device
  convenience cache, keyed by:
  - **Account signature** for broker Formats 1/2 — the sorted, `+`-joined names of the accounts
    selected at load (one holdings file may combine several accounts), so a Roth-only reload and a
    Roth+Individual reload are cached independently.
  - **Year-range signature** (`firstYear-lastYear` + `bracketMode`) for account-less Formats 3/4/5,
    which carry no account info to key on. Survives a filename change or a routine bond rollover
    (a matured TIPS replaced by a new CUSIP for the same funded year) — it only misses if the
    ladder's own year span changes. Two different account-less ladders that happen to share the
    same year span will collide on one cache slot; there is no other signal to key on for these
    formats.
  - The checkbox is off by default and only reflects/writes the key for the **current** load; it is
    never applied automatically. When a saved plan matches, a dismissible banner offers
    "Saved DARA plan found — Apply?"; the load-time mirror is what's shown until the user clicks
    Apply, so §Per-Year DARA from Portfolio's "no inference at load" invariant holds even with the
    cache on. Checking the box saves the store immediately; every subsequent bulk/per-rung mutation
    re-saves; unchecking removes the cached entry (a real opt-out, not just "stop updating").
- **Standalone DARA-plan file** (2.1 §Standalone DARA-plan file) — an export/import of just the
  `#fundedYear,dara` (+ `#splitYears`) block, no CUSIP rows, independent of which broker format the
  holdings came from. The durable, portable path — survives a new machine or browser, unlike the
  cache above — imported as an explicit overlay onto already-loaded holdings, not a replacement
  upload.

**Restoring the "$ each" echoes.** Both Apply (cache) and Import (standalone file) restore
`_daraByYearStore` and `_splitYears`, but neither payload carries `_segInferredEcho` (§UI: segment
actions) — that map only records the outcome of a UI action taken *this* session, not something
either persistence format stores. `_syncSegEchoFromStore` rebuilds it after either restore: for
each resulting segment, if every one of its years shares the same value in the restored store, that
value is shown in the segment's `$ each` box (an even/flat segment has one meaningful figure); a
lumpy segment shows blank rather than a misleading single number, same as a freshly-inferred lumpy
shape would. Requires `_panelYearRange` to already reflect the restored `firstYear`/`lastYear`, so
`renderDaraByYearPanel()` runs once first (which also updates the `rebal-first-year`/`rebal-last-year`
dropdowns for the standalone-file path) before `_syncSegEchoFromStore` computes segments and a
second, cheaper `_renderSegTools()` call displays them.

## Named Quantities & Formulas (Rebalance)

**Funded-Year Quantity Before** (`fyQtyBefore`):
```
fyQtyBefore_b = ROUND((DARA − LMIFromAbove_Before − nonTargetPI) / piPerBond_b)
```
`LMIFromAbove_Before` = sum of annual interest from all actual holdings in years maturing after year b (includes both funded and excess holdings at those years). `nonTargetPI` = P+I from non-target CUSIPs held in the same bracket year. Future30y cover years are excluded — treated as pure excess (`fyQtyBefore = 0`). This formula is applied to ALL standard gap bracket years regardless of CSV format.

**Excess Quantity Before** (`excessQtyBefore`):
```
excessQtyBefore_b = MAX(0, qtyBefore_b − fyQtyBefore_b)
```
Funded-first rule: apply the funded-year claim first; the remainder of the held quantity is the current excess. This *derives* the before-split rather than reading the import's excess column back verbatim — but the **imported excess is not discarded**. It still does its two jobs: in duration matching it is the cover/bracket excess (the recorded build intent), and in funded sizing its annual interest and realized AMD role-play as later-maturity interest (`LMIFromAbove_Before` above counts both funded **and** excess holdings). The *size* of the imported cover excess is also what recovers the intended first and last funded year (see 2.0 §Gap Year Coverage Model and §Future 30Y Rungs). Because a correctly-built ladder's derived split reproduces its own imported excess, a build→export→import→rebalance round-trip yields no trades. For Future-30Y cover years: `excessQtyBefore = ROUND(future30yTotalCost × weight_b / costPerBond_b)` (cover-pair cost split).

**Excess Quantity After** (`excessQtyAfter`):
```
excessQtyAfter_b = ROUND(totalBlockCost × weight_b / costPerBond_b)
```
Post-rebalance excess for duration matching. `costPerBond = price/100 × indexRatio × 1000` (per **TIPS Basics**).

**3-bracket lower bracket priority rule** (replaces the weight-based formula for orig lower and new lower):

`targetLowerCost = totalBlockCost × bracketWeights(dur_newLower, dur_upper, dGap).lowerWeight`
(2-bracket formula using only the new lower / Jan 2036 duration)

`totalCurrentLowerCost = excessQtyBefore_origLower × costPerBond_origLower + excessQtyBefore_newLower × costPerBond_newLower`

If `totalCurrentLowerCost > targetLowerCost` (**over-allocated** — Scenario 1):
- Sell orig lower first: `olTargetCost = max(0, origLowerExcessCost − min(origLowerExcessCost, overage))`
- Sell new lower only if overage remains after depleting orig lower: `nlTargetCost = max(0, newLowerExcessCost − remainingOverage)`

If `totalCurrentLowerCost ≤ targetLowerCost` (**under-allocated** — Scenario 2):
- Freeze orig lower at current: `olTargetCost = origLowerExcessCost` (never increased)
- Buy new lower to fill: `nlTargetCost = max(0, targetLowerCost − origLowerExcessCost)`

`excessQtyAfter_origLower = ROUND(olTargetCost / costPerBond_origLower)`
`excessQtyAfter_newLower  = ROUND(nlTargetCost / costPerBond_newLower)`

**Cash Delta** (`cashDelta`, `costDelta`):
```
cashDelta = -(qtyDelta × costPerTips)
  where qtyDelta = qtyAfter - qtyBefore
```
Positive = cash received (net sell); negative = cash outflow (net buy).

## Bracket Identification Rules

Rules differ by bracket mode:

### 2-Bracket Lower Bracket (Canonical)
The lower bracket is **always** the latest Jan TIPS from `tipsMap` with maturity year strictly below `minGapYear` (currently Jan 2036). Holdings are not consulted. This is the same search used for `anchorBefore` in gap parameter calculation. Any older holdings that previously served as the lower bracket (e.g. Jan 2034) are no longer in `bracketYearSet`; Full rebalance rebuilds them to funded-year qty only, selling any excess.

### 3-Bracket Original Lower Bracket (Holdings-Based)
For the original lower bracket only, the identification is holdings-based:
1.  **CUSIP Aggregation**: Sum total quantity of each CUSIP across the entire portfolio.
2.  **Excess ARA Priority**: The primary metric is **Excess ARA** (`araByYear[y] - DARA`). The year with the greatest positive difference is the original lower bracket. **Note**: "Excess ARA" is a one-time empirical probe used solely here — not a general concept.
3.  **Tie-Breaking**: Among candidates with equal Excess ARA, pick the CUSIP with the highest aggregate quantity.
4.  **Range**: Only years in `[LOWEST_LOWER_BRACKET_YEAR (2032), minGapYear)` are considered.
5.  **Fallback**: If no holdings-based lower bracket is found (e.g. `firstYear` inside the gap), `identifyBrackets` falls back to the tipsMap canonical search — same result as 2-bracket.

## Rebalance Algorithm Flow

This is *the* rebalance algorithm (there is one rebalance — the "Gap-only" mode it was once named for was scrapped; see §Purpose). It runs the full ladder including the gap brackets, and is driven by the **per-year DARA targets**, which may be uneven (2.0 §Duration Matching).

### Phase 1: Derive Parameters
- Settlement date, RefCPI, per-year DARA targets.
- Build holdings ladder and identify official range.
- **DARA shape**: the per-year targets come from the panel store (mirror at load, or user-reshaped). For the default mirror, Run applies the **shape-preserving self-financing scale** (see §Funding the rebalance) so the executed targets net to non-negative cash; an explicitly reshaped store / our-export DARA block is used as-is.
- Identify current gap years.

### Phase 2: Missing Block Parameters
- Calculate **Gap Avg Duration** and **Gap Total Cost** (see **2.0 TIPS Ladders**).
- If `lastYear > 2056`, calculate **Future 30Y Avg Duration** and **Future 30Y Total Cost**.

### Phase 3: Bracket Resolution
- Identify lower/upper bracket CUSIPs from holdings per §Bracket Identification Rules.
- Calculate modified duration for each bracket.
- Solve weights using **2.0 TIPS Ladders** (2-bracket or 3-bracket mode).
- If `lastYear > 2056`, identify Future 30Y cover pair and solve weights.

### Phase 4: Ladder Rebuild (longest to shortest)
Process ALL years in [firstYear, lastYear] **longest to shortest**.
- **Bracket years**: use BEFORE-state LMI pool to ensure `fyQty` remains constant.
- **Rebalance years**: use running post-rebalance LMI pool.
- Apply bracket excess quantities on top of target `fyQty`.
- **LMI pool rule**: Process according to **2.0 TIPS Ladders §Algorithm**. Excess bracket holdings are actual bonds — their interest reduces funded-year quantities of all shorter rungs, on equal footing with funded-year holdings.

## Excess Balance Check

A **diagnostic** of how well the *current* holdings pre-funded the gap coverage — **not** the funding invariant. The funding invariant is **net cash (`costDeltaSum`) ≥ 0 and small**, guaranteed by the self-financing scale (§Funding the rebalance).
```
1. Previous excess cost
2. Cost to fill rebalance years
3. Gap theoretical cost

Surplus/(Shortfall) = 1 - 2 - 3
```
Near-zero indicates the portfolio already held the bracket excess that the gap requires. **Large negative is expected for a basic file** (broker/legacy CSV) that was never built by this app and so never pre-funded the gap — it simply means the rebalance must buy that excess now. The rebalance still self-finances: the shape-preserving scale sells the funded rungs down to raise the cash, so `costDeltaSum` lands small/non-negative even when this surplus is deeply negative. Read this check as "did the prior portfolio carry its gap coverage," and read `costDeltaSum` as "is the rebalance self-financing."
