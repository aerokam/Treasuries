## TIPS Ladder Terminology

**[DARA (Desired Annual Real Amount)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#dara):** Target total cash flow for a funded year in real (inflation-adjusted) terms
- Replaces DAA from 2.0 Bond Ladders
- "Real" means in today's dollars

**[ARA (Annual Real Amount)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#ara):** Actual cash flow produced by the ladder for a funded year in real terms
- Replaces AA from 2.0 Bond Ladders
- May differ from DARA due to rounding

**Note on "Real":** All principal and interest values are inflation-adjusted, so "real" is implied throughout

## Gap Years

**[Gap Years](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#gap-years):** Years within the ladder period where TIPS have **not yet been issued** (currently 2037, 2038, 2039) — they will be issued in later years; until then the block is covered synthetically (build) or by bracket excess (held ladder).

**Synthetic TIPS for Gap Years:**
Created to maintain ladder continuity and facilitate duration matching.
- **Maturity**: January 15 of the gap year (10-year TIPS are issued in January and July).
- **Index ratio**: 1.0 (par assumption).
- **Price**: 100 (par).

**Gap Parameter Calculation:**
Synthetic TIPS are processed **longest to shortest** to model a hypothetical ladder as if gap TIPS existed. This establishes the **Gap Total Cost** (used for bracket sizing) and **Gap Avg Duration** (used for duration matching).

**Anchor when firstYear is inside the gap:** `anchorBefore` is always the nearest Jan TIPS with a year strictly below `minGapYear`. When `firstYear = 2038` or `2039`, `minGapYear` is 2038 or 2039 (no 2037 or 2038 Jan TIPS exists), so the code walks backward from `minGapYear − 1` until it finds the Jan 2036 TIPS, which is always the correct pre-gap anchor.

**LMI for gap years** uses the same definition as for any other rung: annual coupon interest from ALL TIPS maturing after the gap year. This includes:
- **Actual TIPS LMI**: annual interest from funded years above the gap year. When `preLadderInterest` is active, only funded years with non-zero TIPS purchased contribute (zeroed funded years have no actual TIPS and therefore no coupon income).
- **Synthetic LMI** (`runningSynLMI`): accumulated synthetic interest from longer gap years already processed in this sweep (same accumulator pattern as the main ladder).

**Pre-Ladder Interest (PLI) — single interleaved pass** *(Build and Rebalance, when `preLadderInterest` enabled)*:
The PLI pool is distributed across ALL years in maturity order (short→long), with funded years and gap years interleaved. Gap years consume pool before longer-dated funded years (e.g., 2037–2039 are processed before 2040), so whether a funded year like 2040 gets zeroed depends on how much pool the gap years consumed first.
```
remaining = preLadderPool
for year in allYears ascending (short→long):   // allYears = rangeYears ∪ gapYears, sorted
  if year is a funded year:
    need = MAX(0, DARA - prelim[year].laterMatInt)
    if remaining >= need:
      zeroedFundedYears.add(year)
      remaining -= need
    else:
      partialCreditYear = year; partialCredit = remaining; remaining = 0; break
  if year is a gap year:
    need = MAX(0, DARA - actualTIPSLMI(year))   // full prelim LMI for all funded years above; synth LMI not yet known
    if remaining >= need:
      pliCredit[year] = need
      remaining -= need
    else:
      pliCredit[year] = remaining
      remaining = 0; break
```
After this pass, `zeroedFundedYears` is fully determined (including whether bracket years like 2040 are zeroed).

**Steps** (long→short qty sweep):
1. **Anchors**: `anchorBefore` (latest Jan TIPS before gap) and `anchorAfter` (earliest Feb TIPS after gap).
2. **Interpolate Yield**: Linear interpolation between anchors using maturity dates.
3. **Synthetic Coupon**: `MAX(0.00125, FLOOR(yield * 100 / 0.125) * 0.00125)`.
4. **Duration**: Calculate Modified Duration for each synthetic TIPS.
5. **PI per Synthetic TIPS**: `PI_y = 1,000 × (1 + coupon / 2)` (nPeriods = 1, Jan maturity; in dollars).
6. **Required Quantity**: `qty_y = MAX(0, ROUND((yearDARA_y − runningSynLMI − actualTIPSLMI − pliCredit[y]) / PI_y))` where `yearDARA_y = daraByYear.get(y) ?? DARA`.
   - `runningSynLMI` = accumulated synthetic interest from longer gap years already processed in this sweep.
   - `actualTIPSLMI` = sum of annual coupon interest from all actual funded years above this gap year.
   - `pliCredit[y]` = PLI pool credit allocated to this gap year (0 when `preLadderInterest` is disabled).
   - Synthetic TIPS in ladder 1 are constructed exactly like any other rung: LMI and PLI credit reduce the required quantity the same way they do for actual funded years.
7. **LMI Update**: `runningSynLMI += qty_y × 1,000 × coupon`. This accumulated synthetic interest **does** feed into the `runningSynLMI` term for shorter gap years in subsequent iterations.

## Gap Year Coverage Model

The gap coverage algorithm can be understood as two equivalent constructions:

**Component 1 — Synthetic ladder:**
Every year in the ladder period — funded years and gap years alike — is treated as a rung. Gap years receive synthetic TIPS (hypothetical, never purchased) sized so each gap year delivers ~DARA, exactly like any funded year. Each synthetic TIPS generates annual coupon that flows as LMI to shorter-maturity rungs, including the funded year immediately below the gap block. No excess exists anywhere in this view.

**Component 2 — Bracket excess:**
Same construction, but gap years have no TIPS. The theoretical cost of the synthetic gap TIPS is instead allocated to bracket excess holdings at the lower and upper bracket years, split by duration weight. The excess bonds' annual coupon replaces the LMI that the synthetic gap TIPS would have provided:
- **Upper bracket (2040) excess coupon** matures *after* the gap, so it is paid *through* the gap years (2037–2039). Like every other rung's coupon, it is income arriving in those years and **sizes the gap synthetic quantities down** — credited as LMI to all rungs shorter than 2040, the gap years included.
- **Lower bracket (2036) excess coupon** matures *before* the gap, so it flows only to the 2036 funded year and shorter rungs — never up into the gap years.

**Upper-bracket coupon feedback (fixpoint).** Because the upper-bracket excess *quantity* is sized from the gap's total cost, while that cost depends on the upper coupon credited into the gap, there is a one-step circular dependency. It is resolved by iterating to a fixed point (a shared routine used by both build and rebalance): size the gap → derive the upper excess → add its coupon to the gap's above-LMI → resize, until the upper excess quantity is stable (~2 iterations; the term is ~1% of gap cost). This makes the gap consistent with the rest of the ladder, where all coupon LMI (funded *and* excess) is credited. Omitting it would over-size the gap brackets by ~2 bonds each (~3% of gap cost) — the prior behavior.

A small residual remains: synthetic gap TIPS (par, coupon from yield interpolation) have different coupon rates than the actual bracket TIPS, and the fixpoint rounds the upper excess to whole bonds each step. This keeps the `avgAmt ≈ DARA` coverage invariant within ~0.6%.

**Bracket year ARA vs DARA:**
In the synthetic view, 2036 is a plain funded year sized for `ARA ≈ DARA`. In the actual ladder, the 2036 excess coupon also flows to the 2036 funded year as additional LMI, slightly reducing the funded-year quantity needed. The net effect is that the bracket year's total `ARA = DARA + small delta`, where the delta arises from the coupon rate difference between synthetic and actual TIPS. This is expected behavior, not a bug.

**LMI is always annual coupon interest only — never principal.** P+I (principal + last-year interest received at maturity) is a separate concept applied only to the funded year that holds the TIPS. Excess bracket bonds never contribute their principal to another rung's LMI.

**Excess coupon always flows inward:** Excess bracket coupon always contributes to LMI for shorter-maturity rungs within the ladder. The shortest rung in the ladder has no shorter rungs to receive the flow — the coupon is applied where shorter rungs exist, and there is no "exit" from the ladder.

## Future 30Y Rungs

**Future 30Y Rungs:** Years beyond the last-issued TIPS maturity year (currently 2056). Future 30Y rungs arise when `lastYear > 2056` (max `lastYear` = 2066).

**Hypothetical TIPS for Future 30Y Rungs:**
Processed **longest to shortest** using the longest-dated actual TIPS (currently 2056) as a flat-curve anchor:
- **Anchor yield**: Yield of the 2056 TIPS (flat-curve assumption).
- **Synthetic coupon**: `MAX(0.00125, FLOOR(yield × 100 / 0.125) × 0.00125)` — same formula as gap-year synthetic TIPS. Models the coupon a newly-issued 30Y TIPS would carry (nearest 0.125% below current yield); the 2056 TIPS actual coupon is not used.
- **Maturity**: February 15 of the Future 30Y year (30-year TIPS are issued in February).
- **Index ratio / Price**: 1.0 / 100 (par assumptions).
- **Duration**: Calculate Modified Duration using the synthetic coupon and anchor yield.
- **Quantity and Cost**: Calculated identically to Gap Years (see above) using the flat-curve anchor.

**Recovering the last funded year on import.** The Future 30Y rungs aren't held as TIPS — they are represented by the **cover-pair excess** (excess at the lower cover, 2056, and the upper cover, 2052), recorded in the import's excess column. So the intended last funded year is recovered by **reverse-engineering the Future 30Y duration match**: the cost-weighted split between upper- and lower-cover excess is a pure duration relationship — independent of DARA and increasing with the number of rungs — so the last funded year is the one whose cover split, computed exactly as the build computes it, reproduces the imported split. This is reliable because the excess column is a calculated field populated only by this app's export; the recovered year is user-overridable for the off-nominal case of a hand-edited file. When there is no cover excess, the ladder simply ends at the last issued TIPS. (The gap block recovers its *first* funded year from the same idea; that side can use a simpler excess-to-DARA ratio, but the ratio shortcut does **not** transfer to Future 30Y — its upper cover is deep-discount and a long block carries a large later-maturity-interest cascade — so the duration-match reversal is used here instead.)

## Future 30Y Upper Cover AMD (Accrued Market Discount)

The Future 30Y upper cover (currently the 2052 TIPS) carries a large market discount because its coupon (0.125%) is far below current yields (~2.7%). **AMD is treated exactly like coupon interest from this low-coupon TIPS** (this is **Option C** — even, held-to-maturity). A par bond returns its whole yield as coupon; a zero-coupon bond returns it all as accreted discount; the 2052 sits near the zero-coupon end — almost all of its yield arrives as price accretion toward par, not as the tiny coupon. Under the constant-yield method that accretion **is** interest above the coupon.

**The income is what the full held excess position earns each year — modeled held-to-maturity (settlement → the 2052's own maturity), independent of how many bonds are sold.** Selling some excess 2052s is only the mechanism that turns the accrued discount into spendable cash (proceeds buy the newly-issued 30Y); it is **not** what defines the income, exactly as a coupon is earned on the whole position regardless of which bonds you later sell. The number sold and the income are therefore **decoupled** — the schedule does not deplete the income basis. This AMD cash flow reduces the number of funded-year bonds needed, analogous to LMI from coupon interest.

**Applies to excess holdings only.** Funded-year 2052 bonds are held to maturity and receive full par — their discount is already captured in P+I. Applying AMD to them would double-count it.

### Income each year — interest on the full held position

For each year, the per-bond real price is taken under the constant-yield method, kept in today's real dollars by applying the **settlement** index ratio at every point (nothing projects future inflation, consistent with rate, coupon LMI, and maturity principal elsewhere):
```
date(Y)       = last calendar day of February of year Y       (≈ 30Y issuance / secondary-purchase date)
adjPrice(Y)   = priceFromYield(yield_2052, coupon_2052, date(Y), maturity_2052) / 100 × indexRatio_settle × 1000
adjPrice(settlement) = costPerBond_2052                       // cost basis
adjPrice(Y ≥ maturity_2052) = parPerBond = indexRatio_settle × 1000   // at/after maturity → par
a(Y)          = adjPrice(Y) − adjPrice(Y−1)                   // this year's accretion increment, per bond
```
`a(Y)` is the per-bond interest accrued during year Y — the basis steps up each year to `adjPrice(Y)`, so the next year counts only the next increment (no double-counting).

### AMD income by funded year

```
future30yUpperAnnualAmd(Y) = future30yUpperExQty × a(Y)     for Y in (settlementYear, maturityYear_2052]
                           = 0                                otherwise
```
The income is on the **full, undepleted** excess — `future30yUpperExQty` is constant across years, **not** reduced by any roll. The range runs every year from `settlementYear + 1` through the 2052's maturity year (`settlementYear` itself contributes 0 — no accretion booked at the cost basis). Every funded year in `[firstYear, lastYear]` within that span therefore receives an AMD credit, not just an early sale window.

**Profile: even, gently back-loaded by convexity.** A deep-discount bond accretes slightly faster toward par as it approaches maturity, so `a(Y)` rises modestly over time; the annual AMD is roughly level with a gentle upward tilt. **Conserving:** `Σ a(Y) = parPerBond − costPerBond_2052`, so the lifetime AMD per bond is exactly the total market discount — no residual, no rescaling.

**Use in fyQty formula:** AMD is subtracted from DARA alongside `runningLMI` — it is NOT added to `runningLMI`:
```
fyQty(Y) = MAX(0, ROUND((dara − runningLMI − future30yUpperAnnualAmd(Y)) / piPerBond))
```

**Pre-Ladder Interest:** AMD for years before `firstYear` (i.e. `(settlementYear, firstYear)`) is summed into the PLI pool exactly like coupon interest; the PLI zeroing algorithm credits it to the earliest funded years. `settlementYear` itself contributes 0 (no accretion yet). The pool has **two named sources**, surfaced separately so the credit is fully traceable: `preLadderCouponPool` (`preLadderYears × annual ladder coupon income`) and `preLadderAmdPool` (`Σ AMD(Y)` for pre-ladder years); `preLadderPool = preLadderCouponPool + preLadderAmdPool`.

**Rebalance before-state:** the held quantity differs from the rebuilt `future30yUpperExQty`, so the schedule scales linearly: `amdForQty(Y, qty) = future30yUpperAnnualAmd(Y) × qty / future30yUpperExQty`.

**Display:** `future30yUpperAnnualAmd` is shown as a separate line item ("AMD — 2052 excess") in the Amount popup for each funded year where the in-ladder AMD is non-zero. It does not appear in the LMI line. The **pre-ladder** AMD is not double-shown there; instead the "Pre-ladder credit" line drills (Level-3) into the pool composition, which breaks out **"Pre-ladder AMD (excess 2052)"** as its own line alongside "Pre-ladder coupon interest" — so the AMD that was realized in the pre-ladder years remains visible and traceable, consistent with the ladder's transparency goal.

*Possible future extension: the 2056 cover also carries some discount; AMD could later be modeled for it too. Currently 2052-only, as it is the only cover with a material market discount.*

## Duration Matching (Brackets)

Missing blocks (Gap or Future 30Y) are covered by additional holdings in a **bracket pair** (or **cover pair**). The goal is for the excess holdings to change in value identically to the missing block if rates move.

### Average Block Duration is Cost-Weighted
The representative duration of a missing block (`averageBlockDuration` / `avgGapDuration` / future-30Y `avgDuration`) is the **cost-weighted** mean of its constituent synthetic/hypothetical rung durations — applied to **both** the gap block and the future-30Y block:
```
averageBlockDuration = Σ(rungCost × rungDur) / Σ rungCost,    rungCost = rung.qty × 1000
```
Cost-weighting is required because per-year DARA may be uneven: a more heavily-funded rung carries more cost and must pull the average toward its own duration. (A simple mean would mis-weight uneven funding.) When `Σ rungCost = 0`, fall back to the simple mean of rung durations.

### 2-Bracket Weights (Standard)
```
lowerWeight * d_lower + upperWeight * d_upper = averageBlockDuration
upperWeight = (averageBlockDuration - d_lower) / (d_upper - d_lower)
lowerWeight = 1 - upperWeight
```

**Lower bracket always exists (when gap years are in range):** When `firstYear < 2040` and gap years are present, the lower bracket — the latest Jan TIPS with maturity year strictly below `minGapYear`, found by scanning `tipsMap` — always exists. Currently Jan 2036. When `firstYear` is inside the gap (2037–2039), the lower bracket year (Jan 2036) is below `firstYear`; it is inserted into `yearBondMap`/`rangeYears` before the sweep with `fundedYearQty = 0` (pure bracket excess, no funded year component). Duration-matching always uses the standard 2-bracket formula with this lower bracket. When `firstYear ≥ 2040`, no gap years exist in the ladder range and bracket logic does not apply. **Rebalance**: in 2-bracket mode the lower bracket is always this canonical year, regardless of existing holdings; any prior lower bracket year (e.g. Jan 2034) falls into the normal rebalance sweep and is rebuilt to funded-year qty only.

### 3-Bracket Mode (Rebalance)
Used when holdings contain an "original lower" bracket year (e.g. Jan 2034) alongside the canonical "new lower" (e.g. Jan 2036). Rather than a weight formula, a **priority rule** governs how excess is allocated between the two lower bracket years:

`targetLowerCost = totalBlockCost × bracketWeights(d_newLower, d_upper, dGap).lowerWeight`
*(2-bracket formula using only the new lower / Jan 2036 duration)*

**Over-allocated** (`current2034ExcessCost + current2036ExcessCost > targetLowerCost`):
- Sell Jan 2034 excess first; sell Jan 2036 only if still over after depleting Jan 2034.

**Under-allocated** (`current2034ExcessCost + current2036ExcessCost ≤ targetLowerCost`):
- Freeze Jan 2034 at current excess (never increased); buy Jan 2036 to cover the remainder.

`excessQtyAfter_origLower = ROUND(olTargetCost / costPerBond_origLower)`
`excessQtyAfter_newLower  = ROUND(nlTargetCost / costPerBond_newLower)`

Jan 2034 (orig lower) is identified via holdings-based excess-ARA search (see §Bracket Identification Rules in 3.0). Jan 2036 (new lower) is always the canonical tipsMap pre-gap Jan TIPS. Jan 2034 excess is never increased by a rebalance.

### Future 30Y Cover Pair
When `lastYear > 2056`, two existing TIPS are used to duration-match the hypothetical Future 30Y rungs:
- **Future 30Y lower cover**: Always the 2056 TIPS.
- **Future 30Y upper cover**: The TIPS with the longest modified duration excluding 2056 (currently 2052).

#### LMI at the 2056/Future 30Y Boundary

The 2056 funded year's LMI will appear low — and lower than the LMI shown for the 2057 hypothetical rung — for two structural reasons that are correct, not bugs:

1. **No actual bonds exist above 2056.** Future 30Y rungs (2057–2066) are hypothetical; their coupon income is synthetic, used only to size hypothetical quantities. None of it flows into the actual ladder's `runningLMI` pool.

2. **Duration constraint forces weight into 2052.** The hypothetical future30y TIPS are long-dated (~30–40 year modified duration from settlement). The 2056 TIPS has a higher coupon and therefore shorter duration than needed to match the average. `bracketWeights` must assign substantial weight to the 2052 TIPS (near-zero coupon, acts as a near-zero-coupon bond, long duration) to reach the target. The 2052 excess bonds mature before 2056 and their coupons flow only to years ≤ 2051 — they contribute nothing to 2056's LMI. Only the 2056 excess bonds (the lower-weight fraction) contribute coupon income to 2056.

The apparent inversion — `LMI(2057 hypothetical) > LMI(2056 actual)` — is therefore an apples-vs-oranges comparison: the 2057 figure is synthetic LMI from within `calcFuture30yParams`, while the 2056 figure is actual income from purchased bonds. The 2056 funded year is correctly sized as long as `ARA(2056) ≈ DARA`.

### Excess Quantities
For each bracket `b`:
```
excessQty_b = ROUND(totalBlockCost × weight_b / costPerBond_b)
```
where `costPerBond_b = price/100 × indexRatio × 1000` (per **TIPS Basics** — IR always included). This formula applies to the upper bracket (2040) in both modes, and to all brackets in 2-bracket mode.

**Exception — 3-bracket mode orig lower and new lower:** the priority rule (see §3-Bracket Mode) replaces the weight formula for those two years. The upper bracket (2040) still uses the standard formula above.

### Excess Amount (Gap Bracket Display)
The P+I of the excess bracket bonds alone understates the gap coverage because the synthetic ladder used LMI from longer-dated funded years to size down the gap quantities. That LMI must be added back to recover the full coverage amount:
```
gapLMITotal   = sum over gap years of (laterMatInt_y + pliCredit_y)
excessAmt_b   = excessQty_b × piPerBond_b + weight_b × gapLMITotal
```
where `piPerBond_b` is the bracket bond's P+I per bond (including index ratio). Summing `excessAmt` across both bracket rows gives `gapLMITotal + sum(excessQty × pi)`, which approximates `numGapYears × DARA`. This ensures the total Amount column divided by the number of ladder rungs (including gap years) ≈ DARA.

## Algorithm (Longest-to-Shortest Sweep)

Process actual bonds and funded years **longest to shortest**.

1. **Initialize LMI Pool**: `runningLMI = 0`.
2. **For each year Y (longest to shortest)**:
   a. **Incoming LMI**: Sum of inflation-adjusted `annualInterest` from all bonds maturing *after* Y.
   b. **Bracket Excess Contribution**: If year Y contains bracket excess bonds, their coupon interest contributes to the pool for year Y itself.
      - `excessLMI_Y = excessQty_Y × annualInterestPerBond_Y`
   c. **Target FY Quantity**:
      - `Required_PI = YearDARA - (IncomingLMI + excessLMI_Y)`
      - `fundedYearQty_Y = MAX(0, ROUND(Required_PI / piPerBond_Y))`
   d. **Update LMI Pool**:
      - `runningLMI += (fundedYearQty_Y + excessQty_Y) × annualInterestPerBond_Y`
3. **Gap Years**: Contribute zero to the `runningLMI` pool (no actual bonds held; their role is played by bracket excess in other years).

## Transparency & UI Requirements

Duration matching must be educational and traceable. The UI must provide:
1. **Individual Disclosure**: Every synthetic/hypothetical bond used to calculate the average must be listed with its own Modified Duration.
2. **Derivation**: The "Average Duration" must be explicitly shown as the cost-weighted mean of the listed bonds (weighted by each rung's cost = qty × 1000).
3. **Fulcrum Visualization**: A visual representation showing the bracket durations flanking the average duration.
4. **Cost-to-Quantity Chain**: A breakdown showing how the block's total cost is weighted and then converted to discrete bond units.
