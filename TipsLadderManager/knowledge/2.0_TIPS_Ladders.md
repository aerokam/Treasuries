## TIPS Ladder Terminology

**[DARA (Desired Annual Real Amount)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#dara):** Target total cash flow for a funded year in real (inflation-adjusted) terms
- Replaces DAA from 2.0 Bond Ladders
- "Real" means in today's dollars

**[ARA (Annual Real Amount)](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#ara):** Actual cash flow produced by the ladder for a funded year in real terms
- Replaces AA from 2.0 Bond Ladders
- May differ from DARA due to rounding

**Note on "Real":** All principal and interest values are inflation-adjusted, so "real" is implied throughout

## Gap Years

**[Gap Years](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#gap-years):** Years within ladder period where no TIPS exist (currently 2037, 2038, 2039)

**Synthetic TIPS for Gap Years:**
- Created to maintain ladder continuity
- Maturity: January 15 of gap year (10-year TIPS are issued in January and July)
- Index ratio: 1.0 (assumes refCPI = dated refCPI)
- Price: 100 (par)

---

## Future Years

**Future Years:** Years beyond the last-issued TIPS maturity year (currently 2056) where no TIPS exist. Future years arise when `lastYear > 2056` (max `lastYear` = 2066).

**Synthetic TIPS for Future Years:**
- Maturity: February 15 of the future year
- Index ratio: 1.0 (par assumption, same as gap years)
- Price: 100 (par)
- Coupon and yield: same as the 2056 TIPS — flat curve assumption; no interpolation

**Future Cover Pair:**
Future years are duration-matched using two existing TIPS — a **future cover pair** — analogous to the lower/upper bracket pair for gap years. The goal is identical: the excess holdings change in value as the hypothetical future TIPS would if rates move.

- **Future lower cover**: 2056 (last issued; lower modified duration due to higher coupon)
- **Future upper cover**: the TIPS with the longest modified duration in the market (currently 2052, which has a very low coupon from its 2022 issuance, giving it a longer duration than the 2056 despite maturing earlier)

The 2052's duration exceeds the 2056's because duration depends on the coupon/yield relationship, not maturity alone. A low-coupon bond concentrates cash flows further in the future, extending duration toward maturity. Excess in each cover TIPS is weighted to match the average duration of the hypothetical future year TIPS (same formula as gap brackets). If the future average duration exceeds the upper cover's duration, all excess goes into the upper cover. The future cover pair is independent of the 2037–2039 gap brackets.

## Synthetic TIPS Construction

**Yield Interpolation:**
```
Anchors: Latest maturity before gaps, first maturity after gaps
Example: Jan 2036 and Feb 2040

syntheticYield = lowerYield + (gapDate - lowerDate) * (upperYield - lowerYield) / (upperDate - lowerDate)
```

**Coupon Calculation:**
```
syntheticCoupon = MAX(0.00125, FLOOR(syntheticYield * 100 / 0.125) * 0.00125)
```

## Algorithm

Use 2.0 Bond Ladders algorithm with these modifications:

1. **Add:** Identify gap years, create synthetic TIPS
2. **Substitute:** Use all inflation-adjusted formulas from 2.1 TIPS Basics:
   - Inflation-Adjusted Principal instead of nominal principal
   - Inflation-Adjusted Annual Interest instead of nominal annual interest
   - TIPS-specific Last-Year Interest rules (month < 7 → 0.5, month ≥ 7 → 1.0)
   - P+I per Bond (TIPS) formula
