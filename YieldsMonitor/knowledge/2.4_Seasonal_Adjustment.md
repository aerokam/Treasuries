# 2.4 Seasonal Adjustment (SA) Overlay

## Dependencies
[YieldCurves/knowledge/1.0_Seasonal_Adjustments.md](../../YieldCurves/knowledge/1.0_Seasonal_Adjustments.md) (the SA transform itself — foundational, read first), [YieldCurves/knowledge/2.1_SA_Intuition.md](../../YieldCurves/knowledge/2.1_SA_Intuition.md), [1.0_Operation.md](./1.0_Operation.md) (symbol reliability), [2.1_Time_Series.md](./2.1_Time_Series.md), [DATA_DICTIONARY.md](../../knowledge/DATA_DICTIONARY.md#sa-yield), [TIPS_Basics.md](../../knowledge/TIPS_Basics.md) (yield calculation conventions).

## Purpose
Adds an optional Seasonally Adjusted (SA) yield overlay to the Time Series tab's TIPS charts, for the maturities where the seasonal effect is large enough to matter: **1Y, 2Y, 5Y**. SA is not offered for 10Y/30Y — the effect amortizes with maturity and is negligible there (see YieldCurves/knowledge/2.2_SAO_Residual_Analysis.md).

## The key fact this feature depends on
CNBC's `US1YTIPS`/`US2YTIPS`/`US5YTIPS` symbols are **not** synthetic constant-maturity indices — each is the bid yield of one specific, real TIPS. CNBC's own quote page identifies which one (e.g. `US2YTIPS` = the Jan 2028 0.50% TIPS as of 2026-07-01). This means the exact same Price → SA Price → SA Yield transform YieldCurves applies to actual TIPS applies here directly, using that bond's real coupon and maturity date — no synthetic-bond construction is needed or used.

## Bond identity: today vs. history
- **Today's point**: uses CNBC's live quote REST endpoint (`quote.cnbc.com/quote-html-webservice/restQuote/symbolType/symbol`, batched with `|`-separated symbols) for `maturity_date` and `coupon` directly — empirically confirmed to match the actual quoted price (`bond_last_price`) to the cent.
- **Every other (historical) point**: CNBC only exposes each symbol's *current* underlying bond, not a historical CUSIP-per-date mapping. The bond identity is instead reconstructed from `TipsRef.csv` (S2 — the full TIPS auction record: CUSIP, maturity, dated date, coupon, base CPI, term) via the rollover rule below.

## The rollover rule
CNBC does not re-pick the underlying bond continuously (i.e. not "whichever bond is nearest N years from today, recomputed daily"). It only rolls at fixed 6-month checkpoints tied to that tenor's *original* auction cycle:

| Symbol | Tenor | Origin auction months | Checkpoint dates |
|---|---|---|---|
| `US1YTIPS` | 1y | 10-year note (Jan/Jul auctions) | Jan 15, Jul 15 |
| `US2YTIPS` | 2y | 10-year note (Jan/Jul auctions) | Jan 15, Jul 15 |
| `US5YTIPS` | 5y | 5-year note (Apr/Oct auctions) | Apr 15, Oct 15 |

**Algorithm** (`resolveTipsBond` in `src/app.js`):
1. `checkpoint` = the most recent checkpoint date (from the table above) on or before the trade date.
2. `targetMaturity` = `checkpoint + tenor years` (same month/day, N years later — always lands exactly on a real TIPS maturity date since 10yr/5yr-origin notes are issued every 6 months).
3. Look up all `TipsRef.csv` rows maturing on `targetMaturity`. When two CUSIPs share that maturity (a 10yr-origin note and an older, coincidentally-overlapping 20yr-origin note/30yr-origin bond can mature the same date), pick the **most recently dated** one (max `datedDate`) — this is the one CNBC actually quotes.

Because `checkpoint` stays fixed for a 6-month window, the selected bond's actual years-remaining drifts within that window (e.g. for `US2YTIPS`, ~2.0y right after a checkpoint down to ~1.5y just before the next one) rather than being recomputed to stay exactly at the tenor. This matches CNBC's observed behavior, not a naive nearest-maturity search.

**Verified** (2026-07-01, checkpoint = Jan 15 2026 for 1Y/2Y, Apr 15 2026 for 5Y): reproduces CNBC's live mapping exactly — Jan-2027/0.375% (1Y), Jan-2028/0.50% (2Y), Apr-2031/1.25% (5Y).

## The transform, per point
For each historical/current yield quote:
1. `settle` = the quote's trade date, advanced to the next SIFMA bond-trading day (T+1 — see DATA_DICTIONARY.md#settlement-date; holidays from `misc/BondHolidaysSifma.csv`).
2. `mature`/`coupon` = resolved per the bond-identity rule above.
3. `price = priceFromYield(quotedYield, coupon, settle, mature)` (shared/src/bond-math.js — standard Excel `YIELD`-convention bond math, frequency=2, actual/actual; see TIPS_Basics.md).
4. `saPrice = price × (SA_Factor(settle) / SA_Factor(mature))` (SA factors from `TIPS/RefCpiNsaSa.csv`, looked up via `shared/src/ref-cpi.js#saFactorForDate` — exact date if published, else the most recent past occurrence of the same calendar month/day, since the daily SA factor is a slowly-drifting annual pattern and no future values are published).
5. `saYield = yieldFromPrice(saPrice, coupon, settle, mature)`.

## UI
- **Toggle**: "Show SA Yield (1Y/2Y/5Y TIPS)" checkbox in the sidebar. Lazily fetches `RefCpiNsaSa.csv`, `TipsRef.csv`, `BondHolidaysSifma.csv`, and the CNBC bond-metadata endpoint on first use (not on page load) — cached for the session; bond metadata is refreshed on "Refresh Data".
- **Chart overlay**: a solid amber (`#f59e0b`) second line per eligible chart (not dashed — distinguishing color, not line style, per user preference). Tooltip labels it "SA Yield" vs. "Yield" for the raw series.
- **Legend**: a small amber-swatch legend line appears under the checkbox while SA is on, clarifying what the color means.
- **Sidebar**: an additional reading next to each eligible symbol's yield/change (today's SA yield, no "SA" prefix — color and the legend already identify it).
- **Y-axis auto-rescale**: `rescaleYToVisible` includes the SA dataset's values (not just the raw series) when computing visible Y bounds, and toggling the checkbox forces a rescale bypassing any prior manual Y-zoom — otherwise the SA line (which can differ from raw by tens of bps) can render off-canvas or clipped. This bit the feature during development: it looked like "no data" in 2D and "clipped below axis" in 10D, but was actually a Y-bounds bug, not a data bug.

## Known limitations
- **US5YTIPS feed reliability**: the underlying CNBC feed for this symbol is flaky with large gaps (see [1.0_Operation.md](./1.0_Operation.md) symbol-reliability note) — SA is computed for it the same way as 1Y/2Y, but it isn't a priority for correctness/robustness work given the feed itself is the limiting factor.
- **Coverage boundary**: if a trade date's resolved `targetMaturity` isn't in `TipsRef.csv` (predates the reference data, or a future checkpoint's target hasn't been auctioned yet), that point is skipped (gap in the SA line) rather than guessed.
