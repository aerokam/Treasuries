# 4.0 TIPS Ladder Rebalancing

## Dependencies

**Requires:** 3.0 TIPS Ladders

**Inherits all terms from 3.0 TIPS Ladders:**

- DARA, ARA, Gap Years, Synthetic TIPS construction

**Inherits all formulas from 2.1 TIPS Basics:**

- indexRatio, adjustedPrincipal, adjustedAnnualInterest

**Inherits all terms from 2.0 Bond Ladders:**

- Funded Year (FY), Later Maturity Interest

## Purpose

Rebalance an existing TIPS ladder by selling some of the excess holdings from bracket years and buying into formerly-gap years (now available) to ensure duration matching and reduce ARA-DARA gaps across funded years.

## Context

When a TIPS ladder was originally built (e.g., 2023), certain years had no TIPS available (gap years, e.g., 2034-2039). Excess holdings were concentrated in bracket years flanking the gaps to duration-match the missing coverage. Over time, Treasury issues new TIPS filling some former gap years (e.g., 2033-2036 now available). Rebalancing sells excess from bracket years and buys into these newly available maturities.

---

## Rebalancing-Specific Concepts

### Official Ladder Range

- **firstYear**: Earliest year with holdings
- **lastYear**: Latest contiguous year with holdings before any gap above 2040
- Gap years above 2040 truncate the official range (e.g., holdings in 2047 and 2049 but not 2048 → lastYear = 2047)
- Known gap years (2037-2039, no TIPS issued) do NOT break contiguity — **mechanism**: all years ≤ 2040 unconditionally extend lastYear; contiguity is only checked among years > 2040 that actually appear in holdings. Since gap years (2037-2039) have no holdings, they never enter the iteration at all.
- **Non-contiguous TIPS > 2040**: Contribute annual interest to earlier years but are NOT counted as rungs for DARA calculation or ladder range determination

### DARA

DARA is passed as an input parameter to the script (CLI flag `--dara` or interactive prompt). An inferred DARA is also computed from current holdings for validation:

1. Build a temporary TIPS ladder based on holdings, with no special handling for gap years; e.g., all interest from 2040 holdings (FY and excess) contributes to later maturity interest for funded years 2036 and lower
2. Sum ARA for all years in ladder range. numRungs for this calc is lastYear - firstYear + 1
3. Inferred DARA = total ARA / numRungs
4. Both values displayed in summary output for comparison

### Current Gap Years

Years within the official ladder range where **no TIPS exist in market data AND no holdings exist** for that year. Currently 2037, 2038, 2039.

### Bracket Years

- **Upper bracket**: Always 2040 (Feb maturity)
- **Lower bracket** (2-bracket mode): Dynamically identified—the single holding with the most bonds among all maturities in years between `LOWEST_LOWER_BRACKET_YEAR` (configurable constant, default 2032) and the first gap year (exclusive)
- **Original lower bracket** (3-bracket mode): Same identification as lower bracket above — the legacy excess holder from when the new lower did not exist
- **New lower bracket** (3-bracket mode): Always `anchorBefore` — the latest 10-year TIPS just below the first gap year (already computed in Phase 2). Natural lower bracket since gap avg duration falls between its duration and the upper bracket's duration.
- **Bracket maturity**: The specific CUSIP within a bracket year that has the most holdings

### Rebalance Years

Years that will receive buys/sells during rebalancing. Depends on method:

- **Gap-only**: Years between the lower bracket year and the first current gap year, exclusive on both ends. These were gap years when the ladder was originally built but now have TIPS available.
  - Lower bracket 2034, first gap year 2037 → rebalance years: 2035, 2036
- **Full**: All years in [firstYear, lastYear] that are neither bracket years nor gap years. Includes years shorter than the lower bracket as well as years between lower bracket and first gap year.

### FY vs Excess (Bracket Years)

- **FY portion**: Holdings needed to achieve DARA for that year's funded rung
- **Excess portion**: Additional holdings beyond FY, held for duration matching gap years
- **Target FY Qty**: For bracket maturity, calculated using residual P+I after non-bracket maturities in that year are settled
- Calculations use P+I for FY/ARA, $ (cost) for duration matching
- **Gap-only**: non-bracket CUSIPs in bracket year held constant; bracket CUSIP sized to residual
- **Full**: non-bracket CUSIPs in bracket year subject to sell-earliest-first logic (same as non-bracket years); bracket CUSIP sized to remaining residual; only bracket CUSIP can be bought

### Named Quantities & Formulas

**FY Qty** (`fyQty`, `fy_qty` in build-lib):
Target quantity for the funded-year rung — how many bonds needed to produce DARA from this year's P+I plus later maturity interest.

**Excess Qty Before** (`excessQtyBefore`):
```
excessQtyBefore = max(0, qtyBefore - fyQty)
```
Current holdings beyond the FY target. Represents prior-build excess for duration matching. Clamped to 0: in 3-bracket mode the new lower bracket was previously a regular rebalance rung, so its fyQty can exceed current holdings.

**Excess Qty After** (`excessQtyAfter`):
```
excessQtyAfter = ROUND(targetExcess$ / costPerBond)
  where targetExcess$ = gapTotalCost × bracketWeight
```
Post-rebalance excess for duration matching.

**Gap Amount** (`ex_amt` in build-lib; computed inline in rebalance popup):
```
gapAmount = excessQty × piPerBond
  where piPerBond = principalPerBond × (1 + coupon/2 × nPeriods)
               = adjustedPrincipal + lastYearInterest   (equivalent forms)
```
Note: `piPerBond = principalPerBond × (1 + coupon/2 × nPeriods)` is the code form of the spec formula `adjustedPrincipal + lastYearInterest` — algebraically identical.

**Gap Cost** (`ex_cost` in build-lib; computed inline in rebalance popup):
```
gapCost = excessQty × costPerBond
```

**Cost Before** (bracket row, FY portion):
```
costBefore_FY = fyQty × costPerBond
```

**Cost After** (bracket row, FY portion):
```
costAfter_FY = fyQty × costPerBond
```
(FY qty is unchanged before/after; all cost delta is in excess portion.)

**Cost Before/After** (non-bracket row):
```
costBefore = qtyBefore × costPerBond
costAfter  = qtyAfter  × costPerBond
```

**Cash Delta** (`cashDelta`, `costDelta`):
```
cashDelta = -(qtyDelta × costPerBond)
  where qtyDelta = qtyAfter - qtyBefore
```
Positive = cash received (net sell); negative = cash outflow (net buy).

**Amount Before / Amount After** (ARA Before/After — `araBeforeTotal`, `araAfterTotal`):
See Phase 5 below for full component formulas.

## Rebalance Modes

### Gap-Only (existing)
Rebalance only years between lower bracket and first gap year (exclusive). All other non-bracket years use current qty. DARA is user-specified. Algorithm: Phases 1–5 below.

### Full Rebalance — Build-From-Cash (new)
**Concept:** Treat full rebalance as "sell everything, rebuild from scratch." The target is a fresh ladder built with the inferred DARA at the current portfolio's total cash value. Trades are current holdings vs target.

**Algorithm:**
1. Compute `portfolioCash` = sum of (current qty × costPerBond) for all holdings
2. Derive `firstYear` / `lastYear` from holdings (Phase 1 logic below)
3. Binary search DARA: call `runBuildFromScratch(dara, lastYear)` → get `totalBuyCost` → converge until `|totalBuyCost − portfolioCash| < tolerance (~$100`)
4. Target = build output at converged DARA
5. Trades: for each year, `qtyDelta = targetQty − currentQtyForThatCUSIP`; any current CUSIPs not in target are sold in full

**Key properties:**
- Net cash ≈ 0 by construction (build cost = portfolio cash, modulo rounding)
- DARA is always inferred from cash — not user-specified for full rebalance
- Gap years / brackets / duration matching: identical to `runBuildFromScratch` (build-lib.js)
- Non-contiguous rungs (> lastYear): included in portfolioCash but not in the rebuilt ladder (future enhancement)

## 3-Bracket Mode

**Concept:** When anchorBefore (the latest 10-year TIPS below the first gap year) is a rebalance year (formerly a gap year, now available), optionally use three brackets — original lower, new lower, upper — for gap duration coverage. Gap avg duration falls between the new lower and upper durations, making new lower the natural duration partner for upper. Original lower retains legacy excess from when new lower did not exist.

**Weights formula:**
```
w1     = currentExcessOrigLower$ / gapTotalCost   ← FIXED, never sold or bought
w2_raw = (gapAvgDuration − d_upper + w1×(d_upper − d_origLower)) / (d_newLower − d_upper)
w2     = max(0, w2_raw)   ← clamp: if orig lower alone overshoots duration target, skip new lower
w3     = 1 − w1 − w2
targetExcess$: origLower = w1 × gapTotalCost (unchanged); newLower = w2 × gapTotalCost; upper = w3 × gapTotalCost
```

**Phase 4 behavior:**
- Original lower: excess held exactly as-is (never bought, never sold)
- New lower: buy to targetExcess$; if w2=0 (orig lower overshoots), no new lower excess bought
- Upper: buy or sell to targetExcess$ as usual

**Infeasibility (w2_raw < 0):** orig lower excess alone exceeds the duration target. New lower is skipped (w2=0); upper absorbs the remainder. Duration match is approximate in this case.

**Note:** Never sell from orig lower bracket in 3-bracket mode. If the orig lower excess is already large enough that the 2-bracket match is satisfied, use 3-bracket mode only adds new lower on top and is a no-op for orig lower.

**UI toggle:** "2-bracket" vs "3-bracket (original lower + new lower + upper)". New lower bracket CUSIP/year displayed when 3-bracket selected.

## Algorithm Flow (Gap-Only)

### Summary

- **Phase 1 — Derive Parameters:** Fetch settlement date and RefCPI, load holdings, determine ladder range (firstYear/lastYear), identify current gap years, compute inferred DARA
- **Phase 2 — Gap Parameters:** For each current gap year, interpolate yield and compute synthetic coupon and duration. Outputs Gap Avg Duration and Gap Total Cost used for bracket sizing
- **Phase 3 — Bracket Identification & Weights:** Identify lower/upper bracket CUSIPs, compute their durations and weights. Optionally compute before-state excess $ for display/verification.
- **Phase 4 — Ladder Rebuild (longest to shortest):** Standard ladder build using post-rebalance quantities throughout. ALL years in [firstYear, lastYear] processed longest to shortest (including brackets). Bracket years hold targetFYQty + excess per duration weights. Later maturity interest at each step is the running sum from already-processed longer years only.
- **Phase 5 — Verification Output:** Build Before/After ARA table, trades table, weight verification, net cash. Write HTML and CSV

---

### Phase 1: Derive Parameters

```
1a. Settlement date:
    Determined by script—walk back from today until FedInvest returns price data

1b. RefCPI:
    Fetched from TreasuryDirect secindex endpoint for the settlement date

1c. DARA:
    Input parameter (CLI --dara flag or interactive prompt)

1d. Build holdings ladder:
    Process all holdings longest to shortest
    Calculate ARA for every funded year
    Include annual interest from non-contiguous TIPS > 2040

1e. Determine official range:
    firstYear = first year with holdings
    lastYear = last contiguous year before any gap above 2040
    Non-contiguous TIPS > 2040 do NOT extend lastYear

1f. Calculate inferred DARA:
    SUM(ARA for funded years in range) / count of funded years in range
    Display alongside user DARA for validation

1g. Identify gap years:
    Years in [firstYear, lastYear] where no TIPS exist in market data AND no holdings exist
```

### Phase 2: Gap Parameters

Calculate synthetic TIPS for gap years (e.g., 2037-2039 as of Feb 2026):

- **Anchors**: anchorBefore = TIPS maturing in **January** of (minGapYear − 1); anchorAfter = TIPS maturing in **February** of (maxGapYear + 1). Currently Jan 2036 and Feb 2040. Match by exact month (month=1 / month=2) and year.
- Interpolate yields linearly between anchorBefore.yield and anchorAfter.yield using calendar time (maturity dates)
- Set synthetic maturity to **February 15** of the gap year
- Calculate synthetic coupon: MAX(0.00125, FLOOR(yield × 100 / 0.125) × 0.00125)
- Calculate MDURATION for each synthetic using the synthetic maturity and yield
- **PI per bond for gap year qty**: `$1,000 × (1 + syntheticCoupon / 2)`. The `/2` factor is because synthetic maturity is February (month < 7 → lastYearInterest = 0.5 × annual interest).
- Gap Avg Duration = average of synthetic durations
- Gap Total Cost = sum of synthetic quantities × $1,000
- Use target qty for 2040 (not actual holdings) and actual holdings for 2041+
- DO NOT add synthetic gap year interest back to the gap later maturity interest pool

**Note on Gap Total Cost:** This can be larger than expected when years above 2040 are underfunded relative to DARA. Less later maturity interest means more bonds needed for each gap year, increasing total cost.

### Phase 3: Bracket Identification and Weights

```
3a. Identify brackets:
    Upper: 2040 (always Feb maturity)
    Lower: Among holdings in [LOWEST_LOWER_BRACKET_YEAR, firstGapYear),
           pick the single CUSIP with most holdings (by qty)

3b. Calculate MDURATION for each bracket maturity

3c. Weights (2-bracket mode):
    lowerWeight = (upperDuration - gapAvgDuration) / (upperDuration - lowerDuration)  → code: lowerWeight (summary)
    upperWeight = 1 - lowerWeight                                                        → code: upperWeight (summary)
    targetExcess$ per bracket = gapTotalCost × bracketWeight                              → code: bracketExcessTarget[year]

3c-alt. Weights (3-bracket mode): see ## 3-Bracket Mode section above for formula.
    origLowerWeight → code: origLowerWeight (summary)
    newLowerWeight  → code: newLowerWeight (summary)
    upperWeight     → code: upperWeight (summary)
    targetExcess$ per bracket = gapTotalCost × bracketWeight                              → code: bracketExcessTarget[year]

3d. Before-state display only (not used in rebuild):
    Cost per bond = price/100 × indexRatio × 1000
    Current Excess $ = (currentQty - targetFYQty_before) × costPerBond
      where targetFYQty_before uses current-holdings later maturity interest
    Before weights = currentExcess$ / totalCurrentExcess$ for each bracket
```

### Phase 4: Ladder Rebuild (longest to shortest)

Process ALL years in [firstYear, lastYear] **longest to shortest**, including bracket years. Gap years are skipped (no TIPS exist). Maintain a running `laterMatInt` pool — the sum of annual interest from all already-processed (longer-maturity) years using their post-rebalance quantities.

```
Initialize: laterMatInt = 0  (accumulates as each year is processed)

For each year from lastYear down to firstYear (skip gap years):
  1. Later maturity interest = laterMatInt (running total from longer years already processed)
     residual = DARA - laterMatInt  (what this year's own bonds must cover)

  2. Determine post-rebalance qty per CUSIP (depends on method):

     GAP-ONLY:
       - Find target CUSIP: CUSIP with most holdings in that year
       - Non-target CUSIPs: held constant at current qty
       - For non-rebalance years: ALL CUSIPs held constant
       - residual_for_target = residual - SUM(non-target P+I at current qty)
       - targetFYQty = ROUND(residual_for_target / pi_target)
       - Bracket year: postRebalQty_target = targetFYQty + ROUND(targetExcess$ / cpb_target)
       - Non-bracket year: postRebalQty_target = targetFYQty

     FULL (non-bracket year):
       - Sort CUSIPs ascending by maturity date
       - Sell earliest maturities first until own P+I <= residual, or all sold
         (reduce qty of earliest CUSIP as much as needed, then next, etc.)
       - If own P+I < residual after sells: buy additional of latest CUSIP
         additionalQty = ROUND((residual - own P+I) / pi_latest)
       - postRebalQty for each CUSIP = settled qty after above logic

     FULL (bracket year):
       - Sort CUSIPs ascending by maturity; identify bracket CUSIP (most holdings)
       - Apply sell-earliest-first logic to non-bracket CUSIPs only
         (reduce non-bracket CUSIPs until non-bracket own P+I + bracket P+I at current qty <= residual,
          or all non-bracket CUSIPs sold)
       - remaining_residual = residual - settled non-bracket own P+I
       - targetFYQty_bracket = ROUND(remaining_residual / pi_bracket)
       - postRebalQty_bracket = targetFYQty_bracket + ROUND(targetExcess$ / cpb_bracket)
       - Only bracket CUSIP can be bought; non-bracket CUSIPs can only be sold or held

  3. Qty Delta = postRebalQty - currentQty (positive = buy, negative = sell)
     Cost Delta = -(qtyDelta × costPerBond)

  4. Add to laterMatInt pool:
     laterMatInt += postRebalQty × 1000 × indexRatio × coupon
     (for all CUSIPs in this year, using each CUSIP's own postRebalQty)
```

### Phase 5: Verification Output

**Main table columns:**

- CUSIP, Qty, Maturity, FY, Principal FY, Later Maturity Interest FY, ARA FY, Cost FY
- Target Qty, Qty Delta, Target Cost, Cost Delta
- ARA FY (Before), ARA-DARA Before, ARA FY (After), ARA-DARA After

**Sign conventions:**

- Qty Delta: negative = sell, positive = buy
- Cost Delta: positive = cash received (sell), negative = cash outflow (buy)
- Net Cash = SUM(Cost Delta): positive = excess cash, negative = need to add funds

**ARA columns:**

- ARA FY: Current holdings ARA (includes excess bracket bonds at current qty)
- **ARA FY (Before)** (`araBeforeTotal`): Ladder cashflow excluding excess bracket bonds. Computed for the last-in-year CUSIP only:
```
  principalPerBond = 1,000 × indexRatio                         → d.principalPerBond
  nPeriods         = 1 if month < 7, else 2                     → d.nPeriods
  ownPrincipal     = fyQty_before × principalPerBond            → d.araBeforePrincipal
  ownCoupon        = fyQty_before × principalPerBond × coupon/2 × nPeriods  → d.araBeforeOwnCoupon
  laterMatInt      = araLaterMaturityInterestByYear[fy]         → d.araBeforeLaterMatInt
  ARA Before       = ownPrincipal + ownCoupon + laterMatInt     → d.araBeforeTotal
```
  where fyQty_before uses current-holdings later maturity interest (Phase 1 pool).

- **ARA FY (After)** (`araAfterTotal`): Post-rebalance ARA. Same formula using post-rebalance quantities:
```
  ownPrincipal  = fyQty_after × principalPerBond                → d.araAfterPrincipal
  ownCoupon     = fyQty_after × principalPerBond × coupon/2 × nPeriods   → d.araAfterOwnCoupon
  laterMatInt   = yearLaterMatIntSnapshot[fy]  (Phase 4 running pool)    → d.araAfterLaterMatInt
  ARA After     = ownPrincipal + ownCoupon + laterMatInt        → d.araAfterTotal
```
  For bracket years: fyQty_after = targetFYQty (not postRebalQty — excess excluded from own-rung ARA).
  Expected: ARA After ≈ DARA (within ~$1 rounding).

- **ARA-DARA**: Gap between ARA and target — minimizing this is an overarching goal

**Summary table:**

- Parameters (settlement date, refCPI, DARA, inferred DARA, firstYear, lastYear, rungs)
- Gap parameters (avg duration, total cost)
- Bracket details (duration, current/post-rebal/targetFY/excess quantities and costs)
- Weight verification: Target vs Before vs After
- Rebalance years list
- Net Cash

## Weight Verification

```
Before weights: current excess $ / total current excess $ for each bracket
After weights: post-rebal excess $ / total post-rebal excess $ for each bracket
Target weights: from duration matching formula (Step 4)

Expected: After weights ≈ Target weights (minor rounding differences)
Before weights typically show lower bracket overweighted (insufficient rebalancing since build)
```

## Excess Balance Check *(planned, not yet implemented)*

Validates whether brackets were properly funded for the full gap coverage task. Three components:

```
1. Bracket Excess $ (gapExcess):
   For each bracket: (currentQty - targetFYQty) × costPerBond
   Sum across both brackets

2. Cost to Fill Rebalance Years (costForPrevGaps):
   For each rebalance year: targetFY cost - current holdings cost
   Sum across all rebalance years

3. Gap Hypothetical Cost (gapCost):
   Sum of synthetic gap year quantities × $1,000
   (from Phase 2 calculation)

Surplus/(Shortfall) = 1 - 2 - 3

Near-zero: Brackets were properly funded for full gap coverage
Large negative: Brackets underfunded—insufficient excess to cover both
  rebalance year buys and hypothetical gap year needs
Large positive: Brackets overfunded
```

**Note:** Gap Total Cost can be higher than expected when years above 2040 are underfunded relative to DARA (less later maturity interest → more gap TIPS needed → higher gap cost).

## Critical Principles

1. **ALWAYS process longest to shortest maturity** for later maturity interest calculations
2. **Later maturity interest** from years STRICTLY GREATER than current year
3. **Bracket maturity selection**: CUSIP with most holdings is the bracket CUSIP. Gap-only: non-bracket CUSIPs held constant. Full: non-bracket CUSIPs subject to sell-earliest-first; only bracket CUSIP can be bought
4. **Gap-only rebalance years**: always > lower bracket year and < first gap year (exclusive both ends)
5. **Full rebalance years**: all non-gap years in official ladder range (including brackets). Multi-CUSIP years use sell-earliest-first to minimize trades; buys only of latest CUSIP (or bracket CUSIP for bracket years)
6. **Post-rebalance quantities throughout Phase 4**: Later maturity interest at each step uses only the running pool built from already-processed longer years — never current (pre-rebalance) holdings. There is no separate "current holdings baseline + delta" cascade; the pool is built fresh longest to shortest using post-rebalance qtys.
7. **Gap year synthetic interest**: DO NOT add back to gap later maturity interest pool during Phase 1 calculation
8. **P+I for FY, $ for duration matching**: Two different units for two different purposes
9. **Bracket year own-rung ARA**: For a bracket year's own funded rung, use targetFYQty for P+I (not postRebalQty). The excess bonds serve gap duration matching, not that rung's income. The full postRebalQty annual interest does cascade to all earlier years.
10. **Non-contiguous TIPS > 2040**: Contribute interest to earlier years but NOT counted as rungs for DARA or lastYear determination
