The adjusted principal of a Treasury Inflation-Protected Security (TIPS) equals its face value multiplied by the index ratio: the current Reference CPI (Ref CPI, non-seasonally adjusted) divided by the Ref CPI on the dated date (the 15th of the month in which the TIPS was issued). Because the Ref CPI on the dated date is fixed for the life of a TIPS and cancels in the day-over-day percentage change, every outstanding TIPS accrues the same daily inflation adjustment — equal to the daily percentage change in the Ref CPI — regardless of when it was issued. That Ref CPI has a predictable, calendar-driven seasonal component on top of its underlying trend. These tabs walk through how that seasonal component is identified and then stripped from TIPS prices to enable fair yield comparison across TIPS maturing in different calendar months.

CPI-U: Average Month-over-Month % Change by Calendar Month

Each gray bar shows the average month-over-month (MoM) % change in the Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers, non-seasonally adjusted (CPI-U NSA), for that calendar month, averaged across approximately 10 years. Gray dots show individual years. The dashed lines mark the H1 (Jan–Jun) and H2 (Jul–Dec) averages: first-half months are substantially higher, with only November consistently averaging below zero in H2. The green line shows the seasonally adjusted (SA) equivalent, roughly flat across all months by design — seasonal adjustment removes exactly this calendar dependence. The SA Factor chart below quantifies the cumulative effect of this seasonal cycle for each day of the year.

SA Factor = Ref CPI NSA / Ref CPI SA — last 10 years

Dividing NSA by SA isolates the seasonal component of the Ref CPI for each day. Values above 1.0 mean NSA is running ahead of trend (spring/summer); below 1.0 means it's running behind (fall/winter). This ratio is the SA Factor S used to adjust TIPS prices.

SA Factor = Ref CPI NSA / Ref CPI SA (daily) CPI-U NSA / CPI-U SA (monthly, plotted 3 months earlier — shows the lag) S = 1.0000

SA Factor S over a single calendar year — 5-year average

The SA Factor S = Ref CPI NSA / Ref CPI SA, plotted across a single calendar year. Each point is the 5-year average for that month/day. The trough in late winter (Feb–Mar) and the peak in late summer (Aug–Sep) reflect the predictable seasonal cycle of CPI-U. TIPS with January and April maturities sit near the trough; July and October maturities sit near the peak.

SA Factor S over the calendar year — with settlement marker

The SA transform multiplies price by S(settle)/S(maturity), stripping the net seasonal component of the Ref CPI between the settlement date and the maturity month/day. This leaves a price from which the SA yield — reflecting trend inflation only — can be derived. The adjustment is entirely in the stub: over whole years S repeats and cancels; only the fractional-year offset between the settlement month/day and the maturity month/day carries a net effect.
